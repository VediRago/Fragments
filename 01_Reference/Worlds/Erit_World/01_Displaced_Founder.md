# Displaced Founder

## Status

Name not final.

This file defines the displaced child who becomes the historical hinge of Erit's world.

He is not part of the main emotional cast of Fragments, and he should not explain Unity. His purpose is to make Erit's world feel like it has a foundation that grows from the same structural logic as Fragments.

---

## Core Role

The displaced founder is a human child who arrives in Roman history after a light crashes from the heavens.

He is in his early teens.

He comes from a far more advanced human civilization, but the people who find him do not understand displacement. They interpret him through the beliefs, fears, and power structures of their own time.

Over time, he becomes the person whose presence pushes Rome away from the history we know and toward the world that eventually produces Erit, the Devaar family, augmentation, preservation, and the dynastic order.

---

## First Discovery

He is not first found by senators, priests, or generals.

He is found by Servan.

They witness the light fall and discover the child afterward. They bring him to their masters, but their story is treated as a joke, lie, or panic.

When they claim he fell from the sky, the masters do not believe them.

Guards punish the Servan in front of the child.

The child does not understand the language yet, but he understands fear, pain, and injustice.

He cries.

Then he reacts.

He pushes a guard away with a force far beyond what anyone expects. The guard crashes across the room and into the opposite wall.

This becomes one of the first stories later used to mythologize him.

---

## Biological Explanation

The founder does not have simple superpowers.

His body comes from a harsher survival reality where humans were always on edge. Their stress response developed differently.

His adrenaline is stronger, lasts longer, and can create extreme bursts under fear, panic, threat, or protective instinct.

The important rule is:

> His strength is not constant power. It is an extreme survival response.

This keeps him vulnerable.

He is still a child. He can be frightened, confused, used, trained, loved, and shaped by the world that takes him in.

---

## First Lesson From Rome

The first thing Rome teaches him is not family.

It teaches him hierarchy.

He sees that powerless people can be punished for telling the truth.

He sees that power decides what is believed.

This becomes one of the early pressures that shapes him.

Later, when he becomes ruthless, that ruthlessness should not feel like it appears from nowhere. It begins here: with fear, helplessness, violence, and the realization that truth without power can be crushed.

---

## Origin Wound

He comes from a human civilization where natural fertility has been lost.

Children are developed in artificial wombs rather than carried through natural pregnancy.

He cannot conceive.

After arriving in Rome, he is taught language, reading, writing, culture, household life, love, and legacy.

Rome gives him a dream his origin world had already broken:

- to belong to a family
- to create a family
- to continue through children
- to pass something forward through blood and memory

This wound becomes one of the deepest roots of the world he later helps create.

---

## The Family That Takes Him In

The founder is taken in by a wealthy Roman family.

They protect him, teach him, train him, and use him.

This relationship should remain complicated. They may give him shelter, language, education, and a sense of belonging, but they also understand that his existence can become power.

They do not simply adopt a lost child.

They turn him into a future.

The family's ambition becomes one of the reasons his personal wound expands into world history.

---

## Slow Decline Toward Ruthlessness

The founder does not begin as a monster.

He begins as a displaced child trying to survive.

Over time, several pressures build:

- the violence of Roman hierarchy
- the memory of his own survival-driven origin
- the need to control fear
- the desire to belong
- the desire for family and legacy
- the realization that power can protect what truth cannot
- the belief that systems can prevent collapse
- the frustration of wanting something he cannot have
- the awareness that the family who took him in is also using him

He spends much of his life on edge.

He wants to release that pressure.

Eventually, he does.

His release is not a single outburst. It becomes political, dynastic, and historical.

---

## The Dynasty Plot

The founder does not create the five dynasties through his own children.

He cannot conceive.

The five dynasties come from the wealthy family that took him in and used him.

That family branches into five major lines:

- four sons
- one daughter

These lines become the foundation of the later dynastic order.

The daughter's line eventually leads to Erit and the Devaar family many years later.

This keeps the founder's tragedy intact. He wants family and legacy, but becomes the tool another family uses to create theirs.

---

## Story Function

The founder gives Erit's world a human foundation.

He explains why the world does not simply feel like Roman inspiration.

It is Rome changed by displacement, myth, survival biology, family longing, power, and the need to make continuity permanent.

He also turns the statue in Sera's prose into a treasure map.

At first, the statue is world texture.

Later, it can be understood as evidence of the displaced child whose wound shaped the world Erit inherits.
