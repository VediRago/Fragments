# House Ventari — Founding Era

## Purpose

This file explains the founding-era authority structure of House Ventari.

It should prevent confusion between:

- what House Ventari does in the Founding Era
- what later lines become after the Fracture Era
- what House Devaar becomes much later

## Core Cause

The Founding Era happens because of a plot Ecalus Ventari starts.

Ecalus takes control of Viriatus after the child is found.

House Ventari hides the truth of Viriatus's arrival because revealing it would make the boy impossible to keep.

If the public knew he came from a light in the heavens, rival powers, old Rome, religious voices, terrified citizens, and ambitious families could all try to claim, destroy, worship, or control him.

Hiding the truth lets House Ventari own the interpretation of Viriatus before anyone else can.

House Ventari turns him into:

- weapon
- proof
- advantage
- myth
- political leverage

The original goal is not to create a moral order.

The goal is control.

House Ventari uses public displeasure with the old Rome-derived order to justify reshaping it.

Viriatus becomes the proof that Ventari discipline can create what the old order failed to protect.

Empiran is the later word for the empire that grows from this transformation.

Regulatus is the order used to control the masses inside it.

Over generations, Ventari expansion and Regulatus control allow the dynasty to control the world.

## Name Bridge

The four founding Ventari siblings are also keys.

Together, their first letters form **ERIT**:

- **E**rmerus Ventari
- **R**utare Ventari
- **I**lunis Ventari
- **T**eresta Ventari

This creates a hidden bridge between the oldest dynastic structure of the world and Erit herself.

**Erit** carries the sense of **will be / shall be**.

This mirrors **Será** in Portuguese, which also means **will be**.

So Sera and Erit are linked through the idea of becoming, future state, and what something will become.

The four founding lines also become bridges to Erit because their initials form her name.

This is not meant to be public in-world knowledge by default.

It is a structural key for the world and the story.

## Public Justification Method

House Ventari's public justification follows the core method.

They do not publicly justify their rise by revealing Viriatus's hidden origin.

They use the old order's visible failures instead.

### Experience

The old Rome-derived order is corrupt, starving, losing important lands, being seized by enemies, and failing to protect families.

Officials protect themselves while ordinary people bury sons, lose homes, and suffer hunger.

### Emotion

The public emotional wound is:

- fear
- hunger
- humiliation
- resentment
- betrayal
- grief
- exhaustion

### Reason

The reasoning House Ventari encourages is:

> If the old order cannot feed us, defend us, protect our families, or hold our lands, then it has lost the right to rule.

This reasoning matters because it makes Ventari ambition look like public rescue.

The people do not need to know the truth of Viriatus's arrival.

They only need to believe that the old order failed and that Ventari discipline succeeded where the old order collapsed.

### Behavior

People become willing to accept Ventari discipline because it promises:

- protection
- food
- stability
- victory
- order
- survival

This acceptance gives House Ventari the emotional permission to reshape the old order.

### Public Claim

House Ventari's public argument becomes:

> Old Rome failed its people.  
> House Ventari disciplined the impossible.  
> Viriatus conquered because Ventari order gave him purpose.  
> Therefore Ventari order should rule what Viriatus won.

This is not yet full Regulatus.

It is the political and emotional seed that Regulatus later makes permanent.

## Relationship To Regulatus

House Ventari does not follow Regulatus the way ordinary citizens are expected to follow it.

House Ventari uses Regulatus.

That distinction matters.

To citizens, Regulatus becomes the public system that teaches obedience, gratitude, usefulness, hierarchy, belonging, and pride.

To House Ventari, Regulatus is a tool that turns public belief into predictable behavior.

Regulatus makes control feel like duty.

It makes hierarchy feel like protection.

It makes obedience feel like gratitude.

This is how a family crime becomes a civic system.

## Viriatus And The Sibling Threat

Viriatus is not an external threat to House Ventari.

He strengthens House Ventari's public rise.

He never escapes the family physically.

The danger is inside the family.

After Ecalus begins treating Viriatus like the son he wished he had, the Ventari siblings feel displaced by someone they do not see as blood.

After Ecalus dies, public adoration makes Viriatus the obvious figure around whom the reshaped Rome could gather.

To the siblings, this is intolerable because:

- Viriatus is not their blood
- Ecalus seemed to care for him more than them
- his public meaning could outrank their inheritance
- the future after Ecalus might gather around him instead of them

The murder is therefore driven by jealousy, blood legitimacy, succession pressure, and the siblings' desire to control what comes after Ecalus.

Ermerus shapes Ilunis's wound into duty.

Ilunis kills Viriatus.

Ermerus turns the death into Regulatus.

## Founding Authority Map

### Ecalus Ventari

Ecalus starts the plot.

He hides the truth of Viriatus's arrival and publicly frames Viriatus as proof that House Ventari can shape rare power through discipline, education, hierarchy, and purpose.

At first, Ecalus uses Viriatus as weapon, proof, and advantage.

This works because Viriatus gives Ecalus something the old order cannot explain or reproduce.

Ecalus can present House Ventari as the only family capable of turning dangerous power into useful order.

Later, Ecalus begins seeing Viriatus as the son he wished he had.

That emotional shift wounds his actual children because it changes Viriatus from a tool of the family into the child who seems to receive the father's pride.

That wound creates the conditions for the later murder.

### Viriatus

Viriatus is the center of the founding plot.

He is not the creator of Regulatus.

He is the power, symbol, and public myth House Ventari builds around.

His victories make Ventari control possible because they let the family claim that discipline created success.

His death makes Regulatus possible because Ermerus can turn grief into doctrine after Viriatus is no longer alive to become the center himself.

### Ermerus Ventari

Ermerus does not take an institution in the Founding Era.

He controls interpretation.

He controls both brothers by understanding what they need to believe.

He turns Ilunis's jealousy into family duty because Ilunis can act violently if his wound is given a noble reason.

After the murder, Ermerus controls the aftermath and turns public grief into Regulatus.

His authority is meaning, not office.

### Rutare Ventari

Rutare does not found bureaucracy yet.

He survives through procedure.

Because he is the protected heir, he is expected to inherit authority but not allowed to practice the danger, command, or consequence that would make him strong.

Because he is anxious, guilty, and unable to face the truth directly, he records, orders, files, counts, and formalizes.

In the Founding Era, this makes him useful to the family structure.

Later, his line can turn procedure into administration because Regulatus needs daily machinery to make myth unavoidable.

### Ilunis Ventari

Ilunis does not found law yet.

He becomes force inside the family structure.

Because he is overused, maimed, humiliated, and then directed by Ermerus, he becomes the person who acts when the siblings need Viriatus removed.

His violence is not random cruelty.

It grows from the belief that being used should have earned him place, and that someone outside the bloodline received what his sacrifice did not.

Later, his line can turn force into law, punishment, military authority, and sanctioned violence because Ilunis needs force to mean order rather than humiliation.

### Teresta Ventari

Teresta does not become a ruling support in the Founding Era.

She learns the truth through Rutare and distances herself from House Ventari.

Her refusal creates the beginning of a rejected line because she will not stabilize the family structure built from the murder.

Her daughter-line becomes vulnerable because distance from the family center means weaker protection, weaker legitimacy, and easier exclusion by harsher branches.

House Devaar does not exist yet.

Devaar belongs much later, after rejection, time, medicine, preservation, chips, augmentation, and bodily dependency.

## Founding Chain

> Ecalus starts the plot  
> -> House Ventari hides Viriatus's origin to keep control of his interpretation  
> -> old Rome-derived failures create fear, hunger, humiliation, and resentment  
> -> House Ventari uses public displeasure to justify reshaping the old order  
> -> Viriatus becomes weapon, proof, advantage, and myth  
> -> House Ventari publicly claims that Ventari discipline gave Viriatus purpose  
> -> Viriatus becomes beloved and powerful  
> -> Ecalus begins loving him as the son he wished he had  
> -> the Ventari siblings feel displaced by someone they do not see as blood  
> -> after Ecalus dies, Viriatus becomes the obvious public figure for the reshaped Rome  
> -> Ermerus turns Ilunis's jealousy into duty  
> -> Ilunis kills Viriatus privately  
> -> Ermerus controls the aftermath  
> -> public grief becomes Regulatus  
> -> AVD begins when Regulatus is declared  
> -> Regulatus consolidates mass control inside the emerging empire  
> -> Empiran becomes the later word for that empire

## Constraint

Do not treat the Founding Era as a clean institutional chart.

This is not yet a stable system.

It is a family plot, public displeasure, a hidden crime, and a public interpretation that becomes world order.
