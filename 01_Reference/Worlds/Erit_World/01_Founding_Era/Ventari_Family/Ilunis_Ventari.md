# Ilunis Ventari — Founding Era

## Core Identity

Ilunis is the second son of House Ventari.

He is overused because he is strong.

Rutare is overprotected.

Ilunis is overused.

## Core Experience

Ilunis is maimed at the Battle of Augunar while defending Ventari lands from invaders.

He falls from his horse and is crushed in the battle commotion.

His lower body is permanently damaged.

After Augunar, he can barely walk.

## Core Wound

Ilunis feels that he threw his life away for House Ventari and still lost his place beside his father.

Viriatus becomes the living proof that sacrifice did not earn him what he wanted.

## Inner Logic

> Mercy did not save my body.  
> Softness did not protect the lands.  
> Pain is the cost of order.  
> If something threatens the bloodline, it must be cut out.

## Behavior

After Augunar, Ilunis becomes ruthless.

His humiliation turns into control.

His later logic becomes:

- law as restored control
- punishment as proof of strength
- force as cure for humiliation
- weakness as danger
- betrayal as something that must be cut out

## Founding Era Role

Ilunis kills Viriatus privately after Ecalus dies.

Viriatus is vulnerable, kneeling over Ecalus's casket as a grieving son.

Ilunis stabs him in the back.

Killing Viriatus gives Ilunis power again.

## Later Consequence

The Ilunis line can become tied to law, punishment, judgment, military authority, and sanctioned violence.

The contradiction:

> The Ilunis line punishes betrayal while descending from betrayal.
