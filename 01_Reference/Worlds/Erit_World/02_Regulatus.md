# Regulatus

## Purpose

This file defines **Regulatus**, the inherited doctrine of ordered legacy that shapes Erit's world.

Regulatus is not only a law code, religion, government policy, or institution.

It is a method of life.

It is the feeling of being proud to belong to the order that protects, feeds, educates, disciplines, records, and gives purpose to its people.

It becomes one of the main tools used to keep society stable while concentrating power around ruling structures.

---

## Core Definition

Regulatus is inherited legacy on the surface, but mass control underneath.

It teaches that a good life is ordered, grateful, useful, loyal, disciplined, and controlled.

People are not only expected to obey Regulatus.

They are taught to feel honored by it.

The danger is that Regulatus makes obedience feel like pride.

---

## Core Theme

The core theme of Regulatus is:

> Pride being used to create oppression.

Regulatus does not only push people down.

It teaches people to feel proud of accepting the shape built for them.

That is why it spreads so easily.

From the inside, it does not feel like oppression.

It feels like:

> I am proud.  
> I belong.  
> My family matters.  
> My suffering has meaning.  
> My obedience protects everyone.  
> I am worthy because I accept my place.

Fear can control behavior, but pride controls identity.

---

## Origin

The displaced founder later mythologized as **Viriatus** becomes the first stone of the Empiran order that would one day teach the world to live by Regulatus.

He does not create the full system alone.

His life, myth, wound, discipline, fear, victories, usefulness, and death are taken by **House Ventari** and turned into something larger.

House Ventari hides his true arrival and sells his transformation.

They do not publicly claim:

> We found him.

They create the historical message:

> We formed him.

This lets House Ventari claim authorship over Viriatus.

Because they claim authorship over the conqueror, they later claim ownership over what he conquers.

Regulatus is formalized after his death as the doctrine that turns his memory into control.

---

## Public Meaning

To most people, Regulatus is not brainwashing.

It feels like civilization.

It tells people:

- the Empiran order protects you
- order keeps you safe
- discipline makes you worthy
- gratitude is a civic virtue
- your house matters because it serves the whole
- chaos is what happens when people forget their place
- to live by Regulatus is to live correctly

People can feel proud to say they live by Regulatus.

That pride is part of how the system survives.

---

## Hidden Function

Regulatus is also a control method.

It teaches people to confuse gratitude with obedience.

It makes hierarchy feel natural.

It makes service feel noble.

It makes inherited imbalance feel like responsibility.

It makes resistance feel selfish, immature, or dangerous.

It does not need to tell every person they are being controlled.

It teaches them that control is what keeps the world beautiful, safe, and meaningful.

Structurally, Regulatus:

- channels obedience upward
- preserves hierarchy
- normalizes inequality
- turns conquered land into inherited right
- makes class position feel moral
- makes dissent feel shameful
- keeps power flowing toward ruling structures

---

## Public Word, Private Meaning

Regulatus works because the same word carries different meanings depending on who uses it.

A normal citizen may say:

> Regulatus protects us.

And believe it.

A rebel may hear:

> Regulatus trains people to love their chains.

And reject it.

A damaged believer may say:

> Regulatus protected my family once.

And still doubt what it has become.

A House Ventari member may say publicly:

> Regulatus protects us.

While privately understanding:

> Regulatus keeps people proud enough not to ask who benefits.

House Ventari knows the reason behind the word.

They play along as if they do not.

This mirrors the private-language structure found elsewhere in Fragments, but with the moral direction inverted.

Phil and Abby:

> surface meaning: sarcasm, correction, joke  
> hidden meaning: affection, ritual, intimacy

House Ventari:

> surface meaning: duty, pride, inherited order  
> hidden meaning: control, theft, manipulation, power preservation

---

## Emotional Control

Regulatus controls masses through what impacts humans most: feeling.

It does not only say:

> Obey because the law demands it.

It teaches:

> Obey because it makes you worthy.  
> Obey because your family will be proud.  
> Obey because gratitude proves maturity.  
> Obey because the nation protects you.  
> Obey because without order, everyone suffers.

This turns emotional needs into control tools:

- belonging
- pride
- shame
- gratitude
- fear
- family honor
- purpose
- safety
- identity

Regulatus is powerful because it does not only control what people do.

It shapes what people feel about what they do.

---

## Dynastic Use

House Ventari uses Regulatus to make its authority feel necessary.

They do not only rule through force.

They rule through belief.

Regulatus allows dynastic power to present itself as duty, inheritance, protection, and continuity.

House Ventari understands that a myth is more useful when people feel grateful for it.

Over generations, Regulatus becomes one of the main reasons people accept the world as the best possible nation that could exist.

Later houses and institutions inherit, adapt, and benefit from the same structure.

By Erit's era, **House Devaar** becomes powerful not because it founded the original ideology, but because it controls the bodily systems people depend on: preservation medicine, augmentation, chips, bodily identification, and treatment access.

Regulatus gives those systems moral language.

---

## System Relationships

Regulatus should not flatten all NPCs into the same type of person.

Different characters relate to it differently.

The current test model for NPCs uses **System Relationship** as the first layer.

Possible relationships include:

- **Believer** — accepts the public meaning sincerely.
- **Rejector** — sees Regulatus as control, brainwashing, or theft.
- **Damaged Believer** — still speaks its language but has lived through something that cracked their faith.
- **Outsider** — does not fully share the system's emotional language.
- **Performer / Manipulator** — knows the public meaning is constructed but performs belief because the performance maintains power, safety, status, or influence.

House Ventari sits closest to Performer / Manipulator.

A normal loyal citizen may be a Believer.

A rebel may be a Rejector.

A harmed clerk, doctor, or guard may be a Damaged Believer.

This makes Regulatus useful for NPC behavior without making every NPC identical.

---

## Social Effect

Regulatus shapes how people understand themselves.

A citizen does not only ask:

> Am I free?

They are taught to ask:

> Am I worthy of what the Empiran order gives me?

This shifts attention away from control and toward gratitude.

It makes people police themselves before the state has to do it.

---

## Useful Phrases

Possible in-world phrases:

- I live by Regulatus.
- My house honors Regulatus.
- Regulatus gave us peace.
- Without Regulatus, we return to chaos.
- A worthy life is a regulated life.
- Order is gratitude made visible.
- A house without order is a house already dying.
- Duty is how memory survives.
- Gratitude keeps the world whole.

These phrases should sound sincere inside the world.

Most people using them should not think they are repeating propaganda.

They should feel they are expressing pride, maturity, and belonging.

When House Ventari uses similar language, the meaning changes because they understand the hidden function behind the words.

---

## Story Function

Regulatus shapes the emotional atmosphere of Erit's world.

It may not always appear directly in the main story, but it explains:

- why people are proud of hierarchy
- why dynastic power feels legitimate
- why gratitude becomes social pressure
- why workers and citizens accept limits
- why family legacy becomes civic duty
- why chips and records can feel protective instead of invasive
- why rebellion can feel immature or dangerous to loyal citizens
- why the Empiran order can be beautiful and controlling at the same time

Regulatus is one of the invisible systems that makes Erit's world feel coherent.

It turns history into behavior.

It turns behavior into atmosphere.

It turns atmosphere into quest pressure.
