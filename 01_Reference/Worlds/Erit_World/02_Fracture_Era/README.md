# 02 — Fracture Era

## Dramatic Function

The Fracture Era follows the irony of Ermerus.

Ermerus helps create the first era by controlling Viriatus's death, turning public grief into Regulatus, and establishing a controlled legacy around the founding lie.

But Ermerus's own legacy becomes uncontrolled.

Because he has many bastard or disputed children, his line fractures. The same questions of blood, legitimacy, and order that he used to control the public later become the justification Rutare and Ilunis descendants use to take power away from his line.

Regulatus does not break in this era.

The Ventari family does not follow Regulatus the way ordinary citizens are expected to follow it.

They use Regulatus to control the masses, justify authority, discipline rival lines, and make their power feel like order.

This era follows the consequences of Ermerus creating controlled public legacy while failing to control his own bloodline legacy.

## Core Question

> How did Ermerus's controlled legacy become undone by his uncontrolled bloodline?

## Current Focus

- Ermerus creates controlled public legacy through Viriatus's death and Regulatus.
- Ermerus has many bastard or disputed children.
- His line fractures because legitimacy becomes uncertain.
- Rutare and Ilunis descendants use bloodline legitimacy, purity, and protection of order as justification to remove power from Ermerus descendants.
- Rutare's procedural inheritance hardens into administration, records, permissions, and civic machinery.
- Ilunis's wounded logic hardens into law, judgment, punishment, military authority, and sanctioned violence.
- Teresta's daughter-line or descendant line is expelled, pushed out, or denied line authority.
