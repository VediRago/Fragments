# Quest Example — Chip Removal

## Purpose

This file records the first clear example of how Erit's worldbuilding can naturally generate quest design.

The point is not to create a full quest yet.

The point is to show that once the world has enough cause-and-effect structure, quests become easier to create because they come from existing pressure instead of appearing from thin air.

---

## Design Context

This quest example comes from the development of Teresta's founding line.

Teresta's path creates the medical and bodily-control systems that later shape Erit's world:

- rejected daughter-line
- medical research
- human augmentation
- virus and cure crisis
- mass chip adoption
- clinics as control points
- identification through the body
- dependency through medicine

Because of this, a quest involving chip removal feels grounded.

It is not just an errand.

It comes from history, medicine, rebellion, identity control, and the ruling system's need to track bodies.

---

## Quest Seed

A character wants to join the rebels, but they cannot do so while chipped.

The chip keeps them visible to the system.

To join the rebels, they need it removed.

This requires illegal access to a clinic, extraction tool, doctor, or medical lab.

---

## Core Objective

Remove the chip without being captured, exposed, or losing access to future medical support.

---

## Possible Approaches

### Violent Route

The player can kill the guards and force entry into the clinic.

This is direct and fast, but creates consequences:

- higher security response
- possible witness reports
- later hostility from clinic staff
- the doctor may be killed or flee
- future medical opportunities may close

### Stealth Route

The player can wait until the guards change shifts.

They can study the routine check, movement timing, and gaps in security.

This route rewards observation and patience.

### Disguise Route

The player can disguise themselves as a patient.

This allows entry through intake, but creates risks:

- identity scan
- medical questions
- body checks
- chip verification
- needing to improvise under pressure

### Medical Access Route

The player can subdue a doctor and use their access.

This avoids some security systems, but creates moral and practical consequences depending on whether the doctor is harmed, spared, threatened, or convinced.

### Theft Route

The player can sneak in only to steal the extraction tool or medical codes.

This avoids immediate surgery but may require a second step: finding someone capable of using the tool safely.

---

## Consequence Example

If the player kills the doctor, a later quest to upgrade HP, health, or survivability can fail.

The doctor may have been the only person capable of performing that procedure safely.

This is practical consequence, not fake morality.

The world remembers what the player removed.

---

## Systems Involved

This quest touches several world systems at once:

- **Teresta** — medicine, chips, augmentation, clinics, bodily control
- **Ilunis** — guards, punishment, enforcement, anti-rebel response
- **Rutare** — permissions, identity records, procedure, access rules
- **Regulatus** — public belief that chip systems protect society
- **Rebels** — need to become invisible to the system

---

## Why This Matters

Before this worldbuilding work, making quests from thin air felt difficult and fake.

After building the world and the forces that shape its current state, quest creation became more approachable.

The process became:

> world pressure → character need → obstacle → choices → consequence

This confirms the design direction:

> Build the world pressure first.  
> Let quests appear as consequences.

The quest is not random.

It grows from the systems already built.
