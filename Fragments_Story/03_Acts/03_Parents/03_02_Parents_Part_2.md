# 03_Parents — Part 2

## Purpose

This part establishes family life.

The goal is to show Philip, Abigail, and Sera inside ordinary routines before the illness becomes impossible to ignore.

The danger should enter through normal life, not through exposition.

## Characters

- Philip / Phil
- Abigail / Abby
- Sera

## Structure

School → Family Life → TV

The part should begin with something small and personal, then slowly widen toward the larger world.

## Important Details

### School

This includes Sera's first day of school.

Abby takes her there.

Dumdum is introduced here, along with the Dumdum promise.

This should feel like an ordinary parent-child moment first. The promise matters later because it belongs to Sera's early emotional world.

### Groceries

Abby begins experiencing weakness and fainting during everyday life.

The first signs of decline should appear inside normal routine.

That matters because the illness is not introduced as a distant concept. It enters the family quietly.

### TV

Philip questions where Sera's red hair came from because neither side of the family has red hair.

Abby jokingly claims she cheated on him.

Philip jokes that he only married Abby because of her green eyes.

The argument should feel playful, not serious. It is part of their relationship rhythm.

The television introduces reports about a spreading illness.

Abby is annoyed by the constant tragedy reported throughout the city.

The family mostly ignores it.

Philip changes the channel.

A government advertisement promotes colonization of another world.

No colonists have ever returned.

No contact has ever been established with previous expeditions.

Philip distrusts the program because nobody really knows what waits on the other side.

## Prose Notes

This part should balance warmth with quiet pressure.

The family scenes should still feel alive and playful, but the world outside should begin pressing in.

The illness and colonization program should feel like background noise at first, even though both become important later.
