# Bridge Index

This folder tracks structural connections between scenes, characters, motifs, and later payoffs.

A bridge is not a lore explanation. It is a connection that allows one scene, behavior, image, or misunderstanding to carry meaning into another part of the story.

The purpose of these files is to keep Fragments interconnected without forcing those connections into exposition.

## Bridge Types

### Emotional Bridges

Connections that preserve relationship meaning across absence, death, separation, or change.

Examples: family rhythm continuing after loss, affection expressed through repeated behavior, grief carried through objects.

### Behavioral Bridges

Repeated habits, jokes, corrections, speech rhythms, and misnamings that imply connection without shared memory.

Examples: playful correction patterns, Dumdum counting logic, Sera reshaping Kael's name.

### Visual Motif Bridges

Images that return later with changed meaning.

Examples: eyes, windows, mirrors, reflections, sky, light, black, white, grey.

### Structural Bridges

Scene-to-scene or act-to-act connections that make transitions feel inevitable rather than arbitrary.

Examples: Lucreta ending at a doorway followed by Phil and Abigail leaving a church doorway; relocation advertisements later becoming ruined environmental echoes.

### Misdirection Bridges

Places where the story gives the reader real evidence but encourages an incomplete explanation.

The goal is not to trick the reader with false clues. The goal is to let readers notice the right connection while misunderstanding its shape.

### Unity / Ending Bridges

Connections that prepare the reader for Unity, the hospital ending, and the final evidence that something has changed.

Examples: echo patterns, grey eyes, medical light, window/sky imagery, and the final phrase.
