## 2026-05-20 — Ecos de Uma Só Consciência

**Type:** Early concept / editorial synopsis / psychological sci-fi prototype  
**Status:** Historical artifact  
**Current Canon:** No  

### Why Preserved

This appears to be one of the earliest surviving ancestors of Fragments.

It contains an early direct formulation of fragmented identity, recognition without understanding, continuity of consciousness, and the possibility that two apparently separate people may never have been truly separate.

### Historical Punch

The project changed repeatedly.

The question did not.

## 2026-05-20 / Early Phase — Structural Veyra Concept

**Type:** Early structural concept / story-shaping attempt  
**Status:** Historical artifact  
**Current Canon:** No  

### Why Preserved

This concept begins turning the fragmented-consciousness idea into a story with family structure, hidden truth, apparent romance, reader misdirection, technological intervention, psychic instability, and three versions of the same underlying self.

It introduces several ideas that later evolve into Fragments architecture:

- three related fragments
- technology / psychic / connection paths
- romance as misread self-recognition
- parents trying to control what they do not understand
- the need to reveal the truth naturally without exposition

### Historical Punch

This is where the idea started becoming a story instead of only a thought experiment.

## 2026-05-21 — Glass Memory

**Type:** Prototype / convergence imagery / ending-state exploration  
**Status:** Historical artifact  
**Current Canon:** No  

### Why Preserved

Glass Memory preserves early imagery around collapse, rain, the silhouette, recognition, and the phrase "Too late."

It is important because it explores convergence as an emotional image before the later Fragments cosmology fully existed.

### Historical Punch

The image survived long after its original explanation disappeared.
