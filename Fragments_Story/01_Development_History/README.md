# Fragments Development History

This folder preserves the evolution of **Fragments**.

The files contained here are historical development artifacts, not current canon.

They should be preserved as evidence of how the project developed through early concepts, prototypes, outlines, prose fragments, narrative experiments, and design discoveries.

## Purpose

This archive exists to document:

- early story concepts
- abandoned or replaced versions
- recurring ideas that survived across drafts
- structural discoveries
- emotional-image-first development
- narrative iteration over time
- the movement from explanation-driven concepts toward pattern-based storytelling

## Important Rule

Do not treat these files as current reference material unless they are explicitly marked as current.

A file can be valuable even if it is no longer canon.

Its value may be historical, structural, or process-based.

## How To Read These Files

When reviewing an old artifact, ask:

1. What question was this version trying to answer?
2. What ideas survived into modern Fragments?
3. What changed or was replaced?
4. What did this version reveal about the project?

## Preservation Rule

Keep original wording whenever possible.

Do not rewrite old artifacts to match current canon.

If clarification is needed, add a separate note rather than altering the original file.

## Current Development History Structure

- `01_Source_Artifacts/` — original or near-original files and notes
- `02_Timeline/` — dated development timeline and major turning points
- `03_Notes/` — commentary, interpretation, and historical punch notes

## Core Principle

The goal is not to show a clean path from bad ideas to good ideas.

The goal is to show how the same recurring questions evolved:

- What connects separated lives?
- How does recognition happen before understanding?
- What survives fragmentation?
- How can meaning be communicated through evidence rather than explanation?

Fragments did not develop by replacing its center.

It developed by discovering better ways to express it.
