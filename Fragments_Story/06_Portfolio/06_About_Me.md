# About Me

I'm Diogo.

I currently work full-time in glass manufacturing and build **Fragments** outside of work.

I am interested in story, games, character-driven writing, structure, emotional payoff, and the way players or readers discover meaning through experience.

**Fragments** is the project I started on **20/05/2026** to explore those interests.

It began as a personal writing project and became a way for me to practice organizing complex story material and understand how scenes, structure, and emotion can work together.

What drew me toward this kind of work was not only that I enjoy games, although I do. It was discovering how much joy I find in building systems where ideas connect: world details, character behavior, history, structure, and emotional payoff all supporting each other until the setting feels real rather than decorative.

Fragments helped me understand that I am not only interested in lore as background information. I am interested in lore as a system: something that gains meaning when it is tied to discovery, consequence, and payoff. The pleasure for me comes from making separate pieces fit together in a reasonable way when viewed from a distance.

## What I Am Looking For

My goal is to learn from experienced development teams and understand how games are made in a professional environment.

Story and worldbuilding are the direction I want to grow toward, but I am also interested in opportunities that help me learn production, implementation, QA, content structure, and how teams actually build games.

## How I Think About Story

While working on Fragments, I started noticing that complex ideas only matter if they return to people.

The deeper structure is important because it keeps the story consistent, but the reader does not experience the structure first.

The reader experiences characters, scenes, relationships, loss, recognition, and payoff.

That is the part I try to protect when writing.

## What I Am Still Learning

Fragments has made me pay attention to how much a story needs to say.

Sometimes a scene becomes stronger when I remove what is getting in the way.

Sometimes connection is already there before I notice it.

Sometimes the answer is present from the beginning, and recognition comes later.

Those are the kinds of things I want to keep developing as I continue learning.

## Current Status

Fragments is still in development.

This repository shows the project, the prose, the early versions, and the working notes behind it.

It is not presented as finished professional work. It is presented as evidence of how I think, what I am trying to understand, and the kind of work I want to grow into.
