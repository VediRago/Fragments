# Fragments / NOEME

© Diogo Oliveira. All rights reserved.

**Fragments / NOEME** is an original psychological speculative story project by Diogo Oliveira.

This public-facing overview contains selected non-spoiler development material only. It is meant to communicate the tone, format, and creative direction of the project without exposing the full story mechanics, private system rules, endings, or character secrets.

## What This Project Is

Fragments / NOEME is a dark, emotional, fragmented story about identity, experience, memory, consequence, and the way people are shaped by the conditions they live through.

The story is built through fragments: images, scenes, repeated details, emotional echoes, and character experiences that slowly gain meaning as the larger structure becomes visible.

The project is currently in development. The material here should be understood as selected public-facing development work, not a final released version.

## Tone

The tone is:

- psychological
- emotional
- quiet
- mysterious
- poetic
- unsettling
- character-focused
- symbolic without being random

The goal is not to explain everything immediately. The goal is for the audience to feel the characters first, then slowly understand why the fragments matter.

## Creative Direction

The project uses recurring visual and emotional motifs such as:

- mirrors
- water
- blood
- white light
- black space
- hospital-like spaces
- observation rooms
- bodies and machines
- hands
- faces
- repeated gestures
- memory-like scenes

These motifs are part of the story language. They are not decorative references; they are used to build recognition, emotional pressure, and delayed meaning.

## Characters

The project includes several connected characters, each carrying a different emotional pressure, visual identity, and story function.

Public character material may include selected names, visual references, non-spoiler descriptions, and mood studies. Full character secrets, endings, and private structural roles are not included in this public overview.

## Development Method

The project is built around emotional cause and consequence.

A simplified version of the working method is:

```text
experience -> emotion -> reason -> behavior -> consequence
```

Repeated behavior creates atmosphere. Repeated details become clues. Clues become recognition. Recognition becomes payoff.

The private development material contains deeper system rules and story mechanics. Those private mechanics are intentionally not included here.
