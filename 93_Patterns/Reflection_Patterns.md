# Reflection Patterns

## Core Principle

Reflections in Fragments do not explain identity.

They fail at containing it.

A reflection is useful because the reader already understands what it should do:

- mirror the body
- follow movement
- return the expected face
- remain on the surface

When that behavior fails, the failure reveals instability beneath identity.

## Key Rule

A reflective anomaly does not need to be factual in order to carry structural truth.

Dreams, hallucinations, fear, memory, and perception can all rhyme with the deeper continuity system without becoming literal exposition.

Not every reflection is a cosmic clue.

But every reflection can reveal what a character cannot safely understand yet.

## Kael First Mirror Scene

Kael approaches the observation mirror.

The glare shifts.

She sees herself.

The mirror cracks.

At first, there is no sound.

Only the line opening across her reflection.

Then the wall shatters and the room behind it appears.

### Function

This is a real anomaly.

It establishes that strange phenomena happen around Kael before the story explains psychic ability, displacement, continuity, or Unity.

The surface breaks and another layer appears behind the self.

## Sera Dream Mirror Echo

Later, Sera dreams from a position that resembles Kael's mirror scene.

She is somewhere unfamiliar.

She is wearing a suit that is not hers.

She looks down and begins to understand the body may be Kael's.

Then she reaches the mirror.

The glare shifts.

But the face in the glass is Sera's.

"Me?"

The mirror cracks.

### Function

This does not need to be literal prophecy or direct cosmic transmission.

It can remain a dream.

The dream still carries structural truth because Sera is emotionally processing Kael through identity confusion.

The reader does not need to know what it means yet.

They only need to feel that self and other have become unstable.

## Lucreta Reflection

Lucreta's reflection can linger, delay, or fail subtly before collapse.

### Function

This introduces reflective instability before the reader has any cosmological framework.

At first it reads as illness, fear, or atmosphere.

Later it can be reinterpreted as early continuity disturbance.

## Hospital / Eye Reflection

The hospital ending uses light, eyes, glass, and looking outward rather than a literal mirror.

### Function

The reflective pattern completes itself indirectly.

The girl does not need a mirror to reveal continuation.

Her eyes and the window become the final reflective surface.

## Pattern Logic

```text
Expected reflection
↓
Failure / delay / crack / wrong face
↓
Identity becomes unstable
↓
Another layer is implied behind the visible self
```

## Reader Effect

The reader should first experience reflection anomalies as unease.

Only later should they realize the pattern has been teaching them how identity behaves in Fragments.

## Structural Punch

The mirror does not show who they are.

It shows what identity can no longer hold.
