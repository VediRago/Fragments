



~~***She sits quietly on the corner of her bed. The room dims with passing clouds through the steel framed window. Vera arranges the headdress of white orchids and rosemary, carefully weaving them into her crimson hair.**

Her fingers trace the ornate mahogany at the foot of the bed, following the intricate curves carved into the wood from where they began to where they ended.

Her hand stops.

Her ear turns toward the closed window.

Beyond it, carriage wheels roll across stone.

Voices follow.

She moves her hand to her waist.

Standing, she gently pushes Vera aside. Her stride stiffens, but it is barely noticed.

Vera speaks no words, instead dropping her head as Lucreta passes by.

The outside brims with life.

Carriages rolling across cobbled streets.

The suits and dresses of passing lives.

Wooden caskets stacked with bottles.

Baskets overflowing with fruit.

The parasols.

The hats.

The bags.

The canes.

Lucreta keeps looking as Vera gently pulls her away from the window.

The street slips from view.

Her eyes lift.

Above the rooftops, a bright blue sky.
