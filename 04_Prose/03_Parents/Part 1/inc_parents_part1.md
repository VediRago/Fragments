# ~~***A Chance of Luck**

## ~~***Interlude **

~~***Light cuts through the seams of the old church doors and spills across the dim interior.**

~~***The structure hunches under the shadows of a city that tries to erase the heavens it once sought to reach, weathered and stubborn against the world pushing in from all sides.**

~~***Footsteps cross the empty chamber, and their echo lingers long after the sound has gone.**

~~***The massive doors—separating the quiet within from the restless city beyond—slowly open.**

~~“***You know you have to deal with me for the rest of your life now, right?” the man says.**

~~“***I wasn't aware this was a one-sided deal.” She looks at him. “Are you saying I've been duped?”**

~~***He drops his head.  
“*Guess I need to work on my social engineering.”**

~~***A glance sideways, one brow up.  
“*You caught on faster than I was hoping.”**

~~***She shakes her head.  
“*The skills you should be focused on right now are the ones that'll make that wreck you call home actually livable.”**

~~***He tilts his chin back, grinning.  
“*Two steps out and the demands are already starting?”**

~~***He slows. She doesn't.**

~~“***Am I being duped?”**

~~***She turns back, wind pulling hair across her face.**

~~“***Maybe.”**

~~***The smile comes and goes and she's already walking again.**

~~***The city closes back around them.**

~~***Litter clusters at his clean shoes and he nudges it away without looking down, a wall of cars grinding past in a stream that never thins.**

~~“***So what now? More demands?”**

~~***His gaze drops to her dress. No pockets. The grin settles in.**

~~“***Don't tell me you've got a whole list tucked under your bra.”**

~~***A man in ragged clothes materializes at the edge of their conversation before she can answer.**

~~***He extends a trembling hand.**

~~“***Spare a coin?”**

~~***She glances at the stranger, then back.**

~~“***Could be a sign of good luck.”**

~~***He rubs the back of his head, works through his pockets, comes up empty.**

~~“***I think we're already cursed. You don't happen to have a bill stashed next to that imaginary list, do you?”**

~~“***If I was trying to avoid bad luck,” she says, “do you think I would've married you?”**

~~***The beggar holds his hand out.**

~~***Sirens thread through traffic somewhere distant.**

~~***The city offers nothing quieter than this.**

~~***She reaches up and unclasps the necklace from her neck. Sets it into his palm.**

~~***He stares at the weight of it..**

~~***A gentle nudge shifts her aside as a hand smacks the beggar’s shoulder.**

~~“***Looks like your day just turned around.”**

~~***She turns back as the old man’s shadow drops away.**

~~“***See? Good fortune. Forever. Guaranteed.”**

~~***Leaning slightly in, she adds  
“*And do you really need an imaginary list as an excuse to stare at my chest?”**

~~***He smirks.  
“*Did you say good luck?”**

~~***Tires scream.**

~~***A car locks up six feet ahead and his arm is already across her, bracing.**

~~***Horn blast, then the car punches through and it's gone.**

~~***They look at each other.**

~~“***You sure this ‘good fortune forever’ thing is operational?”**

~~***He glances back at the road.  
“*What was that necklace even made of?”**

~~***They move across the street.**

~~“***Let's go. This isn't exactly what I pictured for our first afternoon.”**

~~***She raises an eyebrow.  
“*Really?” The words mute everything around him as they hit.**

~~***He leans toward her ear.  
“*Keep that up and I’m charging you for the ride.”**

~~***He offers her his arm as they step onto the street, eyes ahead, moving through the surge of bodies weaving in every direction. Buildings rise around them like something grown rather than built, concrete spilling toward a horizon that keeps retreating, jagged edges channeling a rare breeze through the sun-hammered city.**

~~***She glances sideways at him, one eyebrow lifting. “A quiet spot, huh? Bold claim — considering all this.”**

~~***Mischief tugs at the corner of his mouth. “You doubt me? Maybe you should —if that necklace hadn’t bought us a ridiculous amount of luck. Must’ve been worth a fortune.” The grin spreads.**

~~***A sharp cry slices through the crowd’s low roar, and her attention snaps to it. She drifts right, nearly colliding with someone. His hand closes around her arm before she goes far.**

~~“***Car’s just ahead,” he says. She keeps craning toward the noise, nearly stepping into a man cutting the opposite way. “Hasn’t even been a day, and you’re already making a run for it.”**

~~***The words reach her a beat late. She blinks, turns to him. “What?”**

~~***The noise swells; he raises his voice over it. “I was expecting an… ‘Are we there yet?’”**

~~***She tilts her head. “Are you calling me impatient?”**

~~“***Me?” The spark behind his eyes sharpens. “You’re suggesting I go around implying things about people?”**

~~“***People?” she challenges, arching an eyebrow.**

~~“***No.” Quick. Grinning. “Just you.”**

~~***Laughter breaks between them, light and out of place against the city’s grinding pressure. He guides her through the last press of elbows and shoulders. The car comes into view. She moves fast. Doors close in unison; the street noise drops to a muffle. They sit for a moment in sudden silence.**

~~***Hands on the wheel, he turns toward her. “Ready for an adventure, my lady?”**

~~“***Adventure.” She arches an eyebrow. “That’s it? I expected a hotel — a good one — maybe a spa, a proper dinner somewhere that doesn’t make me want to cry.”**

~~***His head dips; a faint sniff. “Caught. Suppose it won’t be a surprise anymore — you just married a man with one kidney, no liver, half a lung, and I’m still eight thousand and forty-two credits short.”**

~~***She sets a hand on his shoulder. With a tone of mock sadness, she says, “Poor man.” Her voice jumps. “So, since this isn’t exactly what I pictured for our first afternoon married — what’s left?”**

~~***She props her chin on her hand, eyes sliding toward him. “Will it at least be… magical?”**

~~***He tries for serious, fails. “The only magic you’ll witness today is me, sugar puffs.”**

~~***A laugh shoots from her. “Sugar puffs? Just — drive, get us out of here.”**

~~“***Your wish, your command.” Perfectly straight face. “Commander.”**

~~***He maneuvers out of the parking spot and her head drifts sideways, settling against the window. Thousands of people move just beyond the glass. None of them look at her. None of them look at each other.**

~~***She's right beside him and he loses her anyway. He glances over just to check.**

~~***"Hungry?" The word lands flat, a small offering.**

~~***She isn't there. Not really.**

~~***He drives. Then his hand finds her leg. "Hey… you okay?"**

~~***Tiredness and something else — she wears both at once. She hears him. But her gaze has already moved somewhere past the question, past him, settling on the crowds outside that streak and dissolve into nothing as the car moves past them” . Her head stays against the window. Her eyes climb slowly from the road below upward.**

~~***"Where do we go from here?" Barely above a murmur.**

~~***"From Narev we head south. Still a bit of a ways." He checks the GPS, then her. "You can sleep if you want."**

~~***The fog sits against every window.**

~~***"We aren't even privileged enough to see the sky." Her eyes don't move from the blur outside.**

~~***He leans forward and looks up out of habit. Fog. Only fog. The city has never been without it — no one alive would know the difference.**

~~***He gets it. He knows exactly where she’s going. He lets it go, letting silence take the stage.**


~~***An hour since she's asleep. the brightness of the daylight thins into something softer, and the city fills the gap, street-glow pressing back against the dark before it can settle. Night comes here but never completes itself.**

~~***"I'm hungry," she says, reaching for the radio.**

~~***"How could you not be." Worry in his voice, but sitting underneath something lighter.**

~~***"You've been asleep for two days."**

~~***"Two days?!" She's already digging for her phone.**

~~***The panic passes. She looks out the window, gets her bearings. "How are we not there yet? Did you sleep too?"**

~~***"You think?" Fast. Ready. "If I had, you'd be losing your mind right now." A sideways glance. "I didn't sleep, and here you are anyway." His eyebrows go up. He's pleased with himself.**

~~***"Find me food or I become your nightmare."**

~~***"Your nightmare?" One eyebrow.**

~~***"I'm too hungry for this. Just — food." She waves him off.**

~~***"I was waiting to see if you'd work it out yourself." Eyes forward, perfectly even. He knows what comes next.**

~~***Flat eyes. No expression.**

~~“***What?”**

~~“***You already are.**


~~***Night takes them fully as they push along roads that no map bothers to name. The city's light refuses to die behind them, bleeding at the horizon. Even out here, the noise lives somewhere behind the ears.**

~~***She's on her phone, half-reclined, thumb moving on autopilit, when his voice detonates beside her.**

~~***"Are—" she flinches hard, nearly dropping it. "—eeeee yyoouuu readddyyy? Don't miss tomorrow — the fight between Jo—"**

~~***"Stop!" The word out before she's decided to say it.**

~~***She exhales through her nose. I wonder if this will someday become annoying.**

~~***"Yeah, I know. I hate long drives too.**


~~***The night envelops everything, except for the headlights, which reveal the dirt road coming to an end.**

~~“***For a while now, you’ve been privileged and didn’t even notice,” he murmurs carefully, keeping his tone neutral to avoid giving anything away.**

~~***Her eyes snap to him, and in a sudden motion, her finger lets the phone slip, as if it had never mattered. Her head turns toward the window, and through the reflection in the glass, he catches her eyes glistening.**

~~“***It’s… vast,” she whispers, unable to form the words. Twenty years of the city’s oppression had erased her notion that there was so much more to life.**

~~***The car comes to a halt, and the motion rocks her sideways, as if she has already drifted to the stars, leaving everything else behind.**

~~***With the engine cutting out and no headlights, the universe engulfs them.**

~~“***I know you won’t enjoy what I’m about to ask,” his voice breaks her spell.**

~~“***I don’t care,” she says, the words slipping out before she can think. No one knows if that’s really what she meant to say.**

~~***At this stage, besides the tiny fragments of light scattered across the sky, nothing is visible.**

~~“***I need you to cover your eyes,” he says, the words heavy with the burden of asking her to leave a moment she’s clearly savoring.**

~~“***I’ve… I’ve covered them,” she replies. She hadn’t heard this serious tone in a very long time.**

~~“***You haven’t tossed a snarky remark in the last fifteen minutes. You’re acting weird,” she adds, a hint of playful accusation in her voice.**

~~“***Hello?” She hears rustling but can’t figure out what the hell is going on.**

~~“***Phil!” she calls. “If I don’t hear a word from you right now, I’ll uncover!”**

~~***Within a second, a whisper brushes against her ear. “I knew you were very impatient.”**

~~***As he’s about to laugh, a smack pushes him away.**

~~“***You ass!” she shouts. “So what’s the—” she drops into a manly voice, “big reveal… oh, I’m sorry, I have to ask you this, blablabla—”**

~~“***Can’t you see?” he cuts in.**

~~“***Can you?” she blurts out.**

~~***He laughs. “I’m naked.”**

~~***A sound tries to escape her mouth, stuttering before it forms. “Are you serious?” she gasps, about to continue when he interrupts and flips on the light.**

~~***In front of her eyes lies a key, resting on the palm of his hand. Though it isn’t clear what it unlocks, she seizes the opportunity to strike back.**

~~“***If you have handcuffs on the other hand, I’ll strap you naked to a tree and leave you to rot.” She glances around before pointing. “There — that will be your tomb, my dearest.”**

~~***He smirks. “This…” He lets the pause stretch, deliberate, before continuing. “Abby… this is our freedom.”**

~~***She now feels as if she’s holding the reins and goes for another jab.**

~~“***Oh… so we are… what, imprisoned? Enslaved?”**

~~***Unlike him, her mask is tight; even though he can see right through her, she plays the furious character extremely well.**

~~***He picks up the loose papers that had scattered while he was searching for them in the darkness.**

~~“***So… should I guess? Will you keep drawing this forever? I’ll have to be at my desk tomorrow, you know?” she rushes him.**

~~“***Even if you tried your best not to, you’d still be the most impatient person I know,” he replies, smirking.**

~~“***Here… maybe I should have told you earlier, so you could have taken in the view. For now, this will be our new home.”**

~~***Cold moves softly around her feet.**

~~***She sits at the edge of the stream with her eyes closed, letting the water drift around her ankles while the morning sun slowly gathers warmth across her skin.**

~~***For once, nothing presses against her.**

~~***No traffic.  
No voices.**

~~***Just water.  
Wind.**

~~“***Not even a good morning, huh?”**

~~***The words reach her through a voice still rough with sleep.**

~~***One eye is all she gives him as her face turns toward his.**

~~“***Good morning to you too, sugar puff puff,” he answers anyway.**

~~“***See? I know you.”**

~~***Her head falls back into the morning light.**

~~“***From the second you wake up, huh?”**

~~***As he turns toward her, something about her leaves him speechless.**

~~“***So… have you thought about it yet?”**

~~***His eyes capture the movement of her lips as those words fall from them.**

~~“***Thought about what? Should I be reading minds now?”**

~~***Then drift toward her feet stirring beneath the surface, the same way she rests beneath the sun.**

~~“***The name.”**

~~***Tying his hands behind his back, he says, “Oh, pfft. Of course. Frisky.”**

~~***As her hands shift behind her back—**

~~“***Ok…” The word falls with the same stillness her feet break.**

~~***He cracks one eye open.**

~~“***What? You asked if—”**

~~“***Sera.”**

~~***He sits up slightly.**

~~“***Sera?”**

~~***Her chest arches toward the sun, embracing the warmth.**

~~“***To be. To become something. Something like that.”**

~~***A quiet stretch settles between them.**

~~“***You didn’t think about it at all either, did you?”**

~~***Her head starts turning toward him.**

~~“***You didn’t even think—”**

~~***A sudden grip pulls her into him, the motion unleashing a laugh that completes the moment.**

~~“***I want those…” Each letter a step as his sight descends toward her lips.**

~~***A forced shake of her head fails to compose her.**

~~“***Do you n—” The words collapse back into laughter.**

~~***A tear slides across her face. “Phil, stop.”**

~~“***I’m not doing anything.”**

~~***He falls back into the grass.**

~~“***I finished my thesis.”**

~~“***The one you tried explaining to me fifty-two times?”**

~~***As she wipes across her eyes—**

~~“***I didn’t understand a word.”**

~~“***That’s because you refuse to appreciate genius.”**

~~“***Fifty-seven.”**

~~“***You started talking about it in tenth grade?”**

~~“***Seventh.”**

~~“***Tenth grade. Wow, that’s been a while.”**
