It’s the rainwater beneath Sera’s feet that detaches her from the flow of bodies around her. Each brush challenges balance; the Next pushes her back to center, fixed on footsteps that ripple puddles that never meet.  
  
A blink. One foot hangs mid-motion.  
  
Another blink.  
  
Her head turns toward the alley the crowd had hidden as realization begins to settle—only to be denied as everything snaps back into motion.  
  
She looks ahead, uncertain which direction still belongs to her. So she walks backwards defying the will to go forward.  
  
It’s now clear her body had to stop at the entrance of the alley.  
  
A passing car splashes her. Warning or push forward—unclear.  
  
It takes no effort to turn toward the darkness. She goes.  
  
Shadow shrinks with each step, pulsing under passing headlights until the final step strikes a bottle.  
  
The sound skitters through the pavement.  
  
And a desperate face emerges from the dark.  
  
Sera’s gaze drops, then drifts upward from blackened feet through a shivering body to a hood beginning to hide eyes turning away from her.  
  
With a quick tilt of her head as her hand lands on her waist—  
  
“You look like this city has given a face.”  
  
Her eyes snap back narrowing at the little girl.  
  
“Oh really?” Shifting her head to the other side while reaching into the opposite pocket—  
  
“There a ton, I mean a TON of loonies around here.”  
  
Sera crawls down to her height giving her a chocolate.  
  
“But you ain’t one of em.”  
  
Smiling when voices appear lingering at the mouth of the alley.  
  
“Hey little girl—”  
  
As the world goes mute the black wall passing by turns white then black. When her head stops there are only bodies.  
  
The sound of a wrapper tingles Sera’s ear.  
  
She’s still holding her breath.  
  
When she decides to face the girl she does it very slowly, standing on the tip of her toes for no good reason.  
  
“and soo... what’s your name—”  
  
As her lips haven’t even touched to finish the sentence eyes widen.  
  
“Kill?!?!” As she looks down to find an extended palm showing an empty wrapper. 0
