# Prose To Write — Priority List

This document tracks which prose scenes are actually needed next.

It is not a full chapter list. It exists to prevent writing random scenes just because they are interesting. A prose scene should be written when it proves a major emotional bridge, fills a structural gap, or supports the portfolio.

## Priority Scale

**Critical** — needed to make the story architecture work.

**High** — strongly supports emotional continuity or portfolio proof.

**Medium** — useful, but can wait.

**Low** — interesting, but not necessary yet.

---

## Critical

### 01 — Parents Part 3 / Abby Gone / Phil Finds Letter

**Why it matters:** This is one of the emotional foundations of the entire story. Abby's absence, the plushy, the letter, the correction ritual, and Phil's collapse establish how love continues through objects and repeated behavior.

**What it must prove:**

- Abby's death is felt through absence, not spectacle.
- Sera unknowingly carries Abby's final words to Phil.
- The letter ends the search and makes hope physically impossible.
- The correction ritual becomes a final expression of love.
- Phil's later actions are driven by Abby's final request.

**Current use:** Portfolio proof for connection without shared memory and emotional continuity after loss.

---

### 02 — Phil Final Lab / Sera Displacement

**Why it matters:** This completes the parents act and explains how Sera survives into the later story.

**What it must prove:**

- Phil's work matters emotionally, not only as plot mechanics.
- The government/colonization system becomes tied to Sera's survival.
- Phil's final act is not heroic spectacle; it is desperation and continuation of Abby's request.
- Sera's displacement feels like a traumatic rupture, not a clean sci-fi transition.

**Current use:** Structural bridge from Parents act into Sera / later world.

---

### 03 — Sera Registration / Chip Alert

**Why it matters:** This scene starts one of the cleanest misdirection chains in the story.

**What it must prove:**

- Sera remained unregistered because her adoptive parents wanted to keep her hidden.
- When they try to sell her, they need to get her chipped.
- The chip system flags an impossible identity conflict.
- The Devaar family is alerted because they have been searching for someone compatible with Erit's condition.

**Current use:** Proof for the Sera alert / Kael false target misdirection bridge.

---

### 04 — Devaar Arrival / Kael False Target

**Why it matters:** This turns the chip alert into a fair misunderstanding rather than a plot convenience.

**What it must prove:**

- The Devaars follow a real signal connected to Sera.
- When they arrive, Kael is visibly symptomatic.
- Sera appears comparatively ordinary.
- The Devaars focus on Kael because she looks like the answer.
- The reader understands why the wrong target is believable.

**Current use:** Proof for the incomplete-information case study and misdirection bridge.

---

### 05 — Erit Body Found / Basement Rupture

**Why it matters:** This is the emotional trigger for the final collapse.

**What it must prove:**

- Sera and Kael interpret the scene through incomplete information.
- Fedra's grief is visible enough that the Devaars do not become simple villains.
- Kael sees enough to believe her worst fear has been confirmed.
- Sera's reaction breaks the room before Kael's outburst breaks the world around them.

**Current use:** Structural bridge into Unity.

---

## High Priority

### 06 — Sera First Day of School

**Why it matters:** This gives Sera ordinary family memory before loss.

**What it must prove:**

- Sera inherits Abby and Phil's emotional rhythm.
- Dumdum logic becomes a family behavior.
- Appearance can be introduced through reflection, reaction, and family rhythm instead of static description.
- The family feels alive before tragedy removes it.

**Current use:** Supports Parents act breathing room and later Sera behavior.

---

### 07 — Parents Bed Conversation / Sera Appearance Reveal

**Why it matters:** This reveals Sera's red hair and green eyes through relationship rhythm.

**What it must prove:**

- Sera visually carries both parents.
- Abby and Phil's teasing rhythm is lived-in.
- Sera becomes visible through family interaction, not exposition.
- The reader later connects Sera visually to Lucreta and Erit.

**Current use:** Supports visual misdirection and family continuity.

---

### 08 — TV Virus Warning / Relocation Advertisement

**Why it matters:** This scene quietly connects the parents' world to later misdirection and Kael's arrival environment.

**What it must prove:**

- The virus exists as a local catastrophe before readers understand the deeper condition.
- The relocation/colonization program feels like a believable public system.
- Phil distrusts it because people do not return.
- Advertisement imagery can later return as ruined environmental echo.

**Current use:** Supports virus misdirection and colonization false theory.

---

### 09 — Kael Arrival / Ruined Advertisement / Mirror Break

**Why it matters:** This introduces Kael's world-shift and early psychic/perceptual instability.

**What it must prove:**

- Kael enters a world the reader already partly recognizes through earlier media/advertisement imagery.
- Kael appears initially healthy or not simply infected.
- The mirror/reflection event hints at psychic instability without explanation.
- The scene bridges Parents world into Kael's arc without a hard reset.

**Current use:** Supports virus misdirection, visual motif bridges, and Kael anomaly setup.

---

### 10 — Erit Deathbed Promise

**Why it matters:** This protects Erit and Kael's relationship from becoming only cure/utility plot.

**What it must prove:**

- Erit values Kael herself, not Kael's usefulness.
- Kael tries to offer herself but cannot express it normally.
- Erit rejects Kael's sacrifice and asks her to promise not to disappear for her.
- The later basement scene feels like a violation of this emotional contract.

**Current use:** Supports Erit/Kael emotional bridge and final rupture.

---

## Medium Priority

### 11 — Kael Literacy / Name Scene

**Why it matters:** This shows Kael's fear of seeming abnormal and builds trust with Sera.

**What it must prove:**

- Kael wants to be seen correctly.
- Learning language is emotionally about normalcy, not only communication.
- Blood or symptoms interrupt the scene without becoming the only focus.
- Sera's response balances concern, humor, and acceptance.

---

### 12 — Kael and Sera Bond-Building Scenes

**Why it matters:** The act needs breathing room before they are taken.

**What it must prove:**

- Their bond grows through humor, miscommunication, curiosity, and trust.
- Kael feels strange but not inhuman.
- Sera uses inherited humor and indirect affection.
- The later loss matters because the relationship had time to breathe.

---

### 13 — Fedra Hugging Sera

**Why it matters:** This protects Fedra from being read as only cold or exploitative.

**What it must prove:**

- Fedra's compassion still exists beneath desperation.
- Sera's resemblance to Erit creates emotional shock.
- Bringing Sera into the family feels emotionally plausible.

---

### 14 — Kael Wakes After Augmentation

**Why it matters:** This establishes Kael's bodily alienation and distrust.

**What it must prove:**

- Kael notices the port/machine connection early.
- Her symptoms may be gone, but her body feels unfamiliar.
- The Devaars' help can still feel violating from Kael's perspective.
- The port later matters in Unity.

---

## Low Priority For Now

### 15 — Additional Ordinary Lives Inside Unity

**Why it matters:** Unity should not feel like only a main-cast montage.

**Why it can wait:** Unity already exists as a strong proof scene. Additional lives can be added later only if needed for pacing and scope.

---

### 16 — Extra Worldbuilding Scenes

**Why it matters:** Some worlds may need grounding.

**Why it can wait:** The portfolio does not need more worldbuilding. Only write these when they support a character, bridge, or act transition.
