# Kael

Kael is not chosen. She becomes the point where several impossible states overlap.

She is psychic, displaced, visibly deteriorating, later altered through augmentation, and eventually connected to Erit's preserved consciousness while still alive. These conditions make her the place where the hidden structure becomes observable.

But emotionally, Kael should not feel like a cosmic mechanism. She should feel like a frightened, lonely girl who believes there is something wrong with her.

---

## Role in the Story

Kael carries the story from personal abnormality into Unity.

She begins as someone who does not fit her own world, then becomes even more displaced after arriving in another. Her body, language, perception, and relationships are all unstable in different ways.

Through Kael, the story explores what it means to be seen as strange, useful, dangerous, or impossible before being seen as a person.

---

## Reader First Impression

Kael should first feel mysterious and unsettling.

The reader does not need to understand her. They should feel that unusual things happen around her: broken reflections, unexplained psychic events, silence, symptoms, and reactions she cannot explain.

Her strangeness should create curiosity before sympathy fully arrives.

---

## Core Emotional Pattern

Kael wants to be normal.

That desire drives much of her behavior. She often pushes through pain, fear, or confusion because appearing strange frightens her more than discomfort does.

She is not simply quiet. Her silence comes from origin, communication difference, fear of being misunderstood, and later distrust.

Kael's emotional pattern is: hide the abnormal, reach for belonging, panic when belonging feels conditional.

---

## Body and Self-Image

Kael views herself as strange, abnormal, and undesirable.

This affects how she interprets attention. If people focus on her symptoms, abilities, or usefulness, she can read that as proof that no one wants her for herself.

After augmentation, this worsens. Waking connected to machines and altered through procedures makes her own body feel unfamiliar. The arm port is important because it first represents violation and alienation, then later becomes part of the Unity mechanism.

Write Kael's body as something she often notices from the outside: unfamiliar, watched, modified, or failing.

---

## Psychic Nature

Kael's psychic abilities should first appear through events, not explanation.

Early signs include broken glass, reflection disturbance, uncontrolled force, and communication that reaches others before anyone understands its source.

Her telepathy is not clean conversation at first. Signals may pulse outward involuntarily and affect nearby people. Sera receives Kael before either of them fully understands what is happening.

The reader should learn the pattern before receiving any explanation.

---

## Language and Literacy

Kael struggles with spoken language and writing.

This is not only a practical obstacle. It exposes her fear of looking wrong.

In the literacy/name scene, the emotional core is not learning letters. It is Kael trying to complete her own name while blood falls onto the page, because finishing the name and appearing normal matters more to her than acknowledging her condition.

Writing Kael should preserve this tension: identity, shame, determination, and bodily failure happening at the same time.

---

## Sera

Sera becomes one of Kael's first emotional anchors.

Sera does not fully understand Kael, but she treats her like someone who can be teased, argued with, pulled along, and included. That matters because Kael expects fear, rejection, or clinical interest.

Their bond begins through partial telepathy, miscommunication, and Sera's refusal to treat Kael as only an anomaly.

Sera gives Kael a first experience of belonging that is messy, imperfect, and alive.

---

## Erit

Erit represents the life Kael believes she should have had.

Healthy, loved, beautiful, normal, included.

At first, Kael's attachment to Erit is shaped by awe and longing. Erit seems to hold the kind of life Kael imagines was denied to her.

The relationship deepens when Kael begins to see Erit's suffering and Erit begins to see Kael beyond usefulness or strangeness. Their bond becomes real because they move past idealization into recognition.

---

## Fedra and Valkare

Kael distrusts Fedra and Valkare because she experiences their help through fear, medical intervention, and incomplete information.

They do not intend to harm her, but Kael sees machines, procedures, altered body, and attention focused on her condition. From her perspective, it can feel like she is valuable only because she may help Erit.

This misunderstanding is important. The tragedy should grow from incomplete information, not villainy.

---

## Unity

Kael becomes the source of Unity because several unstable states overlap through her.

She is not omniscient during Unity. She is overwhelmed.

She can only process continuity one life at a time. Nearby fragments appear more clearly. Distant continuity collapses into speech fragments, images, sensations, and colors.

Dialogue repetition during Unity should be treated as continuity overlap, not normal speech. Someone speaks; Kael echoes. The pattern is evidence of consciousness collision and re-localization.

---

## Writing Notes

Write Kael through perception before explanation.

She should often notice objects, bodies, reflections, light, blood, machines, and silence before she understands what they mean.

Avoid making her explain herself too clearly. Her emotional truth should appear through hesitation, fixation, bodily reaction, involuntary psychic disturbance, and the desperate attempt to seem normal.

Kael is not a lore delivery device. She is the wound through which the larger structure becomes visible.
