

Purpose

Define Kael's behavior throughout the story.

Important

Condition

Kael suffers from the Condition.

Displacement accelerates symptom development.

Self Image

Kael views herself as strange, abnormal and undesirable.

This influences many of her decisions.

Telepathy

Kael possesses psychic abilities.

Many manifestations are initially involuntary.

Communication with Sera begins before Kael fully understands it herself.

Kael cannot completely stop her telepathic broadcasts.

Psychic signals continuously pulse outward and may affect nearby individuals.

Literacy

Kael struggles with language and writing.

Appearing normal matters more to her than avoiding discomfort.

Attachment

Kael becomes emotionally dependent upon Sera.

Erit later represents the life Kael believes she should have had.

Distrust

Augmentation contributes to Kael's distrust of Fedra and Valkare.

Kael often interprets events through insecurity and incomplete information.

Unity

Kael becomes the source of Unity.

Dialogue repetition during Unity should be treated as continuity overlap rather than normal speech.
