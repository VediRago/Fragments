

Purpose

Define Philip and Abigail's behavior throughout the story.

Important

Philip

Philip uses humor and sarcasm to deal with difficult situations.

He enjoys provoking reactions from Abigail.

He is often less emotionally expressive than he appears.

Abigail

Abigail returns Philip's remarks against him.

She uses intentional mistakes and corrections as affection.

She is often more emotionally perceptive than Philip.

Relationship

Philip and Abigail express affection through playful conflict.

There is an unspoken competition between them to win arguments.

Neither allows serious moments to remain serious for long.

Sera

Philip and Abigail love Sera deeply.

Much of their behavior revolves around creating a happy childhood for her.

Loss

Both parents attempt to protect Sera until the end.

Their deaths define the beginning of Sera's journey.
