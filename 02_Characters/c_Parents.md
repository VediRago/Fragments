# Parents

## Purpose

This file defines Philip and Abigail's behavior throughout the story.

Their relationship should feel lived-in before it becomes tragic.

They love each other through teasing, correction, argument, and timing. The warmth is not always direct, but it is always present.

---

## Philip

Philip uses humor and sarcasm to deal with difficult situations.

He enjoys provoking reactions from Abigail.

He is often less emotionally expressive than he appears, but that does not mean he feels less.

His jokes should feel like a way of staying close to people, not pushing them away.

---

## Abigail

Abigail gives Philip's remarks back to him.

She uses intentional mistakes and corrections as affection.

She is often more emotionally perceptive than Philip.

Abigail understands more than she says directly, which makes her final letter matter more.

---

## Relationship

Philip and Abigail express affection through playful conflict.

There is an unspoken competition between them to win arguments.

Neither allows serious moments to remain serious for long.

That rhythm should make their love feel ordinary, specific, and real.

---

## Sera

Philip and Abigail love Sera deeply.

Much of their behavior revolves around creating a happy childhood for her.

Sera inherits pieces of their rhythm without fully knowing it: correction, stubbornness, teasing, and affection hidden inside wrongness.

---

## Loss

Both parents attempt to protect Sera until the end.

Their deaths define the beginning of Sera's journey.

The loss matters because the reader has seen the family rhythm first. What disappears is not only two people, but a whole way of being loved.
