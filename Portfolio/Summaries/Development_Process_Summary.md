# Development Process Summary

Short version of how I am developing **Fragments**.

The early files are not here to show a polished result.

They are here to show what changed.

At first, the project had more direct explanation, more visible setup, and more description around the world.

Over time, I became more interested in the people inside those scenes, and in what the reader could understand through repeated details instead of direct explanation.

## Starting From Images

Fragments began from end-state images and emotional anchors before the full structure existed.

Some early anchors were:

- the hospital girl
- Lucreta
- the silhouette / completed-self image
- collapse and cycle-zero imagery

At first, these images felt connected before I could fully explain why.

The structure grew from trying to understand why they belonged together.

## Less Explanation, More Evidence

One big change was moving away from explaining the hidden structure directly.

Instead of telling the reader what everything means, I started looking for evidence inside the scenes:

- repeated behavior
- familiar images returning later
- small habits that carry emotion
- scenes that feel different after looking back
- endings that show consequences instead of explaining them

That made the story feel less like it was giving answers and more like it was setting up realizations.

## Prose As Part Of The Process

The prose shift became part of the project.

Lucreta and Unity became useful opposite points:

- **Lucreta:** stable reality, physical grounding, clear description
- **Unity:** perception collapse, images, colors, and residue over environment

The prose does not change because of inconsistency.

It changes because reader perception changes.

## Removing Instead Of Adding

One useful lesson came while revising Sera's final scene.

I thought the scene needed more connection.

After removing parts I thought were necessary, the moment felt stronger.

The connection was already there.

That changed how I approached later scenes. I started paying more attention to what earlier interactions were already carrying.

## What I Am Learning

The main lesson is that complexity has to return to people.

Fragments can become complex quickly, with hidden rules, repeated images, and character echoes.

But if an idea does not make the characters or the reader's experience stronger, it does not belong in the story.
