# Method Summary

This is the short version of the method I am currently testing in the Erit worldbuilding branch.

I started with a simple problem: quests, NPCs, and places can feel disconnected when they do not react to what is happening around them. I wanted to test whether worldbuilding could begin from cause and consequence instead of adding lore, factions, NPCs, and quests as separate pieces.

## First Chain

The first chain I used for the foundation of the world was:

Pressure → Experience → Emotion → Reason → Behavior

This was used while building Viriatus, House Ventari, and the early family foundation.

The idea was simple: pressure creates an experience, the experience creates emotion, emotion creates a reason, and that reason creates behavior.

## Atmosphere

Later, while testing the world and factions, I noticed that atmosphere did not need to be forced at the start.

When behavior repeated across a place, group, or institution, atmosphere started to appear by itself.

That changed the chain into:

Pressure → Experience → Emotion → Reason → Behavior → Atmosphere

## Layers

The next problem was that pressure alone was not enough.

The same pressure can affect different people, cities, or factions in different ways. That led to the layer idea.

A layer is the base way something understands pressure.

So the method became:

Layer → Pressure → Experience → Emotion → Reason → Behavior → Atmosphere

Layers helped explain why different NPCs or districts could react differently without making their behavior feel random.

## Locked And Fluid Layers

Not every layer should change.

Some layers are fluid, such as districts, local moods, gangs, crowds, and undecided citizens.

Some layers are locked, such as major factions, institutions, doctrine, and important characters with a fixed role.

This matters because player choice should not make a faction suddenly stop being itself.

## NPCs

For NPCs, the method helped me build characters from the world instead of starting with personality traits.

An NPC can inherit a city layer, a faction layer, a block layer, and a personal layer.

The NPC does not change because a dialogue option says so.

They change only when the experience gives them a new reason to behave differently.

## Quests

For quests, the chain expands again:

Starting Layer → Pressure → Experience → Emotion → Reason → Behavior → Atmosphere → Quest Logic → Player Choice → Consequence → Possible Layer Outcome

A quest should not begin as a random task.

It should come from pressure, behavior, and atmosphere that already exist in the world.

The outcome may reinforce, weaken, or shift a mutable layer, but only after the quest creates an experience strong enough to justify it.

## Current Finding

The main finding so far is that behavior and atmosphere often do not need to be added separately.

When pressure, experience, emotion, reason, and layer are clear, behavior usually becomes easier to find.

When behavior repeats, atmosphere starts to appear.

Quests can then be found inside that atmosphere instead of being placed on top of it.

This is still early and needs more testing, but it is the clearest working method I have in the project right now.
