# Development Diary Summary

Short version of the development diary.

This summary covers the work from the start of Erit's worldbuilding up to the current method tests.

The diary is not meant to read like a finished design document.

It is a record of what changed while I was building, testing, correcting, and reorganizing the project.

## Where The Worldbuilding Started

The Erit worldbuilding started from a practical need:

> How do I make a calendar for Erit's world so I can place the eras in a timeline?

That question led back to a detail already inside **Fragments**: the emperor's statue.

Instead of adding a calendar from outside the story, I used that statue as the seed.

That became Viriatus, House Ventari, the AVD calendar, and the public story of Regulatus.

From there, the world started growing through pressure, family wounds, public memory, and systems of control.

## What Changed While Testing

At first, the work was mostly about making the world feel connected.

Then the same pressure logic started helping with more than history.

It began to shape:

- family lines
- factions
- city pressure
- social structures
- NPC behavior
- quest situations

That was when the work stopped feeling like separate lore files and started feeling like one connected process.

## Layer Discovery

Pressure alone was not enough.

The same pressure can affect different people, factions, districts, or cultures in different ways.

That led to the layer idea.

A layer is the base way something understands pressure.

Layers helped explain why a district, NPC, or faction could react differently without making the reaction feel random.

## Locked And Fluid Layers

Another problem appeared after that.

If every layer could change too easily, the world would feel fake.

If nothing could change, the world would feel static.

That led to the split between locked and fluid layers.

Fluid layers can shift through strong experiences.

Locked layers, like major factions or institutions, should resist sudden change.

That keeps player influence from breaking faction identity.

## NPCs And Quests

The diary also tracks the point where NPC behavior started to appear more naturally.

When layer, pressure, experience, emotion, and reason were clear, behavior became easier to find.

When behavior repeated across a place or group, atmosphere started to appear.

That made quests easier to think about.

Instead of starting with a task, the quest could come from pressure, behavior, and atmosphere already present in the world.

## Current State

The project is still early.

The method still needs more testing.

Right now, the diary is useful because it shows the working trail:

- how the calendar became Viriatus
- how Viriatus became House Ventari and Regulatus
- how factions grew from foundation wounds
- how layers were added
- how locked and fluid layers were separated
- how NPCs and quests started to use the same logic

The current goal is not to prove the method is perfect.

The goal is to keep testing whether world pressure can create behavior, atmosphere, quests, and consequences that feel connected.
