# 05_Erit

## Purpose

This act establishes Erit and her family.

Erit should feel loved before the story turns her condition into a problem to solve.

Fedra and Valkare are powerful, wealthy, and surrounded by advanced technology, but their role here should begin as parents who are afraid of losing their daughter.

## Characters

- Erit
- Fedra
- Valkare

## Structure

Erit → Family → Research → Discovery

The act should move from Erit's home life into the growing fear around her condition, then toward the discovery that pulls Sera and Kael into the Devaar family's orbit.

## World

This part of the story takes place in a world where advanced technology and human augmentation are common.

Most people have identification chips.

Society places little value on ordinary individuals, which makes the Devaar family's wealth and power feel even more distant from normal life.

## Important Details

### Erit

Erit is loved by both parents.

She is raised inside extreme wealth and privilege, but she should not feel like an abstract symbol of that privilege.

She should feel like a child whose life matters deeply to her family.

### Condition

Early signs of the condition begin appearing.

Fedra becomes increasingly worried about Erit's health.

The condition should feel personal before it becomes scientific.

### Research

Valkare uses extensive resources to search for treatments.

Many attempts fail.

No cure has been found.

Research into long-term preservation begins as a contingency if no cure can be found.

This should feel like desperation growing under the surface, not just a technical plan.

### Family

Fedra and Valkare genuinely love Erit.

Their concern for her increasingly shapes their decisions.

This is important because later choices should feel painful and dangerous, not simply evil.

### Discovery

An anomaly connected to a young girl is reported.

The discovery attracts Valkare's attention.

This is the first step toward Sera and Kael becoming connected to Erit's family.

## Prose Notes

The act should balance privilege with fear.

The Devaar household can feel controlled, advanced, and protected, but Erit's condition should make that control feel fragile.

The emotional center is simple: two parents are trying to save their daughter, and that fear begins pushing them toward choices that will hurt other people.
