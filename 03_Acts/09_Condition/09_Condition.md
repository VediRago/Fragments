# 09_Condition

## Purpose

This act establishes the breaking point.

The relationships, research, fear, and hidden choices all collide here. The act should feel like everything has been moving toward this room, even if the characters only understand pieces of it.

The goal is not to explain the whole system. The goal is to make the reader feel the moment when the structure becomes unbearable for Kael.

## Characters

- Kael
- Sera
- Erit
- Fedra
- Valkare
- Devaar family

## Structure

Basement → Outburst → Bodies → Connection

The act should move quickly. Once the basement truth is exposed, there should be very little room for the characters to breathe.

## Important Details

### Basement

Sera and Kael discover Erit's preserved consciousness.

Sera confronts Fedra and Valkare.

Kael discovers Erit.

This is where the hidden choices of the Devaar family become impossible to keep separate from the girls who were affected by them.

### Outburst

Kael loses control.

The psychic event kills everyone nearby.

The outburst should not feel clean or controlled. It should feel like fear, grief, and pressure breaking through all at once.

### Bodies

Kael discovers Sera among the dead.

This is the emotional center of the act.

The reader should understand that Kael is not processing a system anymore. She is seeing the person who mattered most to her lying among the consequences.

### Connection

Kael connects herself to the preservation system.

Unity begins immediately afterward.

This is the first real pause after the outburst. The pause should not feel calm. It should feel like Kael choosing the only action left available to her.

## Prose Notes

The prose should be fast-paced.

Events should happen with little time for reflection.

Movement and emotion are more important than environment.

The connection sequence provides the first pause after the outburst.

The scene should feel like the story crossing a point it cannot return from.
