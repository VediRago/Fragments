# Era One Summary — Founding Era And Year 0 AVD

## Purpose

This file summarizes Era One as the causal story behind the calendar.

It does not replace the detailed Founding Era files.

It exists so the Founding Era can be read as one clear chain from Viriatus's arrival to Year 0 AVD.

Era One ends when Ermerus declares Regulatus.

That declaration becomes Year 0 AVD.

---

## Calendar Rule

Year 0 AVD does not mark the private moment Viriatus dies.

Year 0 AVD marks the public declaration of Regulatus.

This matters because Erit's calendar begins with controlled meaning, not private truth.

The private truth is murder.

The public story is noble death in war.

The calendar begins when House Ventari successfully defines what Viriatus's death means.

---

## Starting Conditions — Before AVD

Before Regulatus, Rome is shaped by sixteen elite houses.

House Ventari is one of those houses.

It is influential, but it is not the wealthiest.

This creates pressure for Ecalus Ventari.

### Experience

Ecalus lives inside a political order where House Ventari has influence but still has to compete with other houses through wealth, marriage, military influence, public reputation, civic authority, and old Roman-derived power.

### Emotion

This creates ambition, insecurity, impatience with divided rule, resentment toward negotiation, and hunger for proof that House Ventari deserves more than influence.

### Reason

Ecalus forms the belief that House Ventari must stop negotiating for power and begin defining power.

### Behavior

Before Viriatus appears, Ecalus uses reputation, discipline, strategy, and public meaning to strengthen his house.

After Viriatus appears, he sees a way to move from influence into dominance.

---

## Viriatus Appears

Servans working under House Ventari see a light appear in front of them.

Then a child appears.

They take the child to Ecalus and say he fell from the heavens.

Ecalus first thinks they are mocking him.

He orders guards to punish them.

Viriatus sees the punishment begin and reacts.

He pushes the guards across the room. One guard hits the wall hard enough to break his shoulder.

Ecalus learns immediately that Viriatus is not normal and that the Servans may have told the truth.

He also learns that visible harm can awaken Viriatus's protective violence.

So Ecalus chooses control through shelter instead of open cruelty.

---

## First Erasure

The Servans are dangerous because they know the true origin.

If their version spreads, Viriatus could become divine, public, contested, or claimed by rival houses, priests, soldiers, or the people.

House Ventari would lose control over his meaning.

Ecalus removes the Servan witnesses quietly.

Viriatus is never told.

This is the first hidden crime of Era One.

It creates the founding pattern:

> lower witnesses see too much  
> -> House Ventari treats their testimony as danger  
> -> the witnesses are removed  
> -> the public receives a controlled story

---

## Public Origin Story

House Ventari does not publicly reveal that Viriatus appeared from a light.

House Ventari does not publicly say:

> We found him.

The public story becomes:

> We formed him.

Publicly, Viriatus is framed as a child House Ventari trained, taught, disciplined, and shaped.

His greatness becomes proof of Ventari discipline.

This public story lets House Ventari attach Viriatus's victories to its own social method.

---

## Ecalus Shapes Viriatus

Ecalus gives Viriatus real things:

- shelter
- food
- language
- education
- discipline
- family structure
- a name
- purpose
- a place in the world

To Viriatus, House Ventari feels like rescue.

To Ecalus, this care is also control.

Viriatus trusts House Ventari because the use is hidden inside belonging.

This matters because Ecalus does not control Viriatus mainly through fear.

He controls him by becoming the source of safety, meaning, and family.

---

## Viriatus Becomes Proof

Old Rome is weak, divided, hungry, corrupt, and unable to protect families.

House Ventari uses public displeasure with the old order to justify replacement.

Viriatus's victories make Ventari discipline look historically proven.

The public message becomes:

> Old Rome failed.  
> House Ventari trained Viriatus.  
> Viriatus succeeded.  
> Therefore, Ventari discipline should replace the old order.

Viriatus conquers under Ventari control.

Ecalus originally dreams of reshaping Rome.

Viriatus delivers Rome, then conquers beyond it.

Rome becomes too small to contain what Viriatus proves possible.

---

## Usefulness Becomes Love

Ecalus does not love Viriatus first.

First, he needs him.

Viriatus gives House Ventari what wealth has not given it:

- inevitability
- conquest
- public awe
- proof of discipline
- defeat of the old order
- expansion beyond Rome
- a future larger than the sixteen houses

Every victory makes Viriatus harder for Ecalus to see as only an asset.

Viriatus delivers everything Ecalus wanted.

Then he delivers more than Ecalus knew how to imagine.

That is how usefulness becomes love.

Ecalus's love begins during the conquest years.

It becomes visible near death.

That difference matters.

By Ecalus's final years, the Ventari children can no longer treat his preference for Viriatus as strategy alone.

They understand that Viriatus is loved.

---

## The Ventari Children

Viriatus wounds the Ventari children because he becomes both emotionally and historically impossible to compete with.

He is not only stronger.

He becomes the child who gives Ecalus the future his bloodline did not provide.

### Ermerus

Ermerus sees Viriatus as proof that blood is not enough.

This is intolerable because Ermerus's later claim depends on blood, legitimacy, and controlled interpretation.

Viriatus threatens Ermerus privately by receiving Ecalus's love and publicly by becoming the obvious figure for the reshaped Rome after Ecalus dies.

### Rutare

Rutare is the protected heir, but protection makes him feel weak and useless.

Viriatus makes him feel smaller, but also treats him as a person.

This creates attachment, guilt, fear, and paralysis.

Rutare later survives pressure through records, procedure, and silence.

### Ilunis

Ilunis is overused because he is strong and later maimed while defending Ventari lands.

He expects usefulness to earn place.

Viriatus receives the pride, public admiration, and historical meaning that Ilunis's sacrifice does not.

Ermerus later turns Ilunis's humiliation into family duty.

### Teresta

Teresta is not part of the murder or immediate cover-up.

She is away when Viriatus dies.

She learns the truth later through Rutare.

Her refusal begins as distance, not open rebellion.

Her line is later pushed outside secure Ventari power.

---

## Ecalus Dies

Ecalus dies before creating the final new order.

His death removes the one person whose authority held the contradiction together.

Before Ecalus dies, Viriatus is useful to House Ventari because his victories prove Ventari discipline.

After Ecalus dies, Viriatus becomes dangerous because people could gather around him as the living center of the reshaped Rome.

The danger is not that Viriatus leaves House Ventari.

The danger is that his meaning outranks the blood heirs.

---

## Private Murder

During family mourning, Viriatus kneels over Ecalus's casket as a grieving son.

Teresta is away.

Rutare witnesses enough to know the official story is false.

Ermerus redirects Ilunis's wound through blood and family duty.

Ilunis stabs Viriatus in the back.

The private truth is:

> Viriatus was murdered by House Ventari because his living public meaning threatened the blood heirs' control of what came after Ecalus.

---

## Public Story

The public story becomes:

> Viriatus died a noble death in war.

This story is false.

It is useful because it gives the public a clean grief object.

It removes family betrayal.

It removes Ilunis's role.

It removes Ermerus's manipulation.

It removes the fact that Viriatus died while grieving the same father the siblings claimed to honor.

The public does not receive a murder.

The public receives sacrifice.

---

## Second Servan Erasure

Servan witnesses are blamed for stealing jewelry during mourning.

This accusation explains confusion around the mourning period through a lower-status crime.

It also removes people who could contradict the family-controlled version of events.

The Servans are killed.

This repeats the origin pattern.

At Viriatus's arrival, Servans see the truth first and are erased so House Ventari can control his origin.

At Viriatus's death, Servans again become disposable so House Ventari can control his meaning.

---

## Regulatus Is Declared

Ermerus turns grief into doctrine.

Viriatus alive could gather loyalty around himself.

Viriatus dead can gather grief around a story Ermerus controls.

The political chain is:

> private murder  
> -> public noble death  
> -> public grief  
> -> doctrine  
> -> Regulatus  
> -> AVD

Regulatus is not created by Viriatus.

Regulatus is created from Viriatus's death by Ermerus.

It turns:

- grief into duty
- admiration into obedience
- loss into gratitude
- hierarchy into protection
- discipline into moral worth
- family control into civilization

To question Regulatus becomes to dishonor Viriatus.

To obey Regulatus becomes to participate in his legacy.

---

## Why This Becomes Year 0 AVD

AVD means:

> After Viriatus's Death

But the calendar does not begin simply because Viriatus privately dies.

The calendar begins when Ermerus publicly declares Regulatus.

Year 0 AVD marks the moment House Ventari successfully controls the public meaning of Viriatus's death.

It is not the year truth happens.

It is the year the official story wins.

---

## Era One Ends With

Era One ends with:

- Viriatus dead
- Ecalus gone
- Ermerus controlling the interpretation
- Ilunis bound to private betrayal
- Rutare carrying guilt and knowledge
- Teresta absent from the first lie and later distanced by the truth
- Servan witnesses erased again
- the public receiving noble sacrifice instead of murder
- Regulatus declared
- Year 0 AVD beginning

House Ventari controls Rome.

House Ventari controls Viriatus's meaning.

House Ventari gives the masses an order it does not obey in the same way.

The family fracture is hidden under public unity.

---

## Era Two Inherits

Era Two does not need to invent a new wound.

It inherits the wound Era One hides.

Regulatus survives.

The public story survives.

The family structure does not remain stable.

Ermerus creates the first era by controlling Viriatus's death and establishing Regulatus, but his own bloodline later fractures through bastard or disputed children.

Rutare and Ilunis descendants use legitimacy, blood purity, and protection of order to take power away from Ermerus descendants.

Era Two begins from this irony:

> Ermerus builds an order by controlling Viriatus's meaning through blood, but his own bloodline becomes the weakness that lets the next fracture begin.

---

## Causal Chain

> House Ventari is influential but not dominant  
> -> Ecalus wants Ventari to define power  
> -> Servans find Viriatus after the light  
> -> Ecalus removes the Servan witnesses and hides the origin  
> -> House Ventari frames Viriatus as formed, not found  
> -> Viriatus's victories prove Ventari discipline in public  
> -> Ecalus's usefulness becomes love during conquest  
> -> the Ventari children feel displaced by someone not seen as blood  
> -> Ecalus dies before creating the final order  
> -> Viriatus becomes the obvious living center of what comes next  
> -> Ermerus turns Ilunis's wound into family duty  
> -> Ilunis murders Viriatus privately  
> -> Servan witnesses are blamed and killed  
> -> public story becomes noble death in war  
> -> Ermerus declares Regulatus  
> -> Year 0 AVD begins  
> -> Era Two inherits Regulatus, the official story, and the unstable bloodline that created both

---

## Consistency Notes

- Viriatus's origin is hidden.
- House Ventari does not publicly reveal that origin.
- Publicly, Viriatus is framed as trained, taught, disciplined, and shaped by House Ventari.
- Viriatus conquers under Ventari control.
- Viriatus does not create Regulatus.
- Ecalus starts the plot but dies before personally creating the final order.
- Ecalus begins loving Viriatus during conquest, but the love becomes visible near death.
- Ilunis kills Viriatus privately after Ecalus dies.
- Ermerus controls the aftermath and declares Regulatus.
- Year 0 AVD begins when Regulatus is declared, not when Viriatus privately dies.
- The public story is noble death in war.
- The private truth is murder during family mourning.
