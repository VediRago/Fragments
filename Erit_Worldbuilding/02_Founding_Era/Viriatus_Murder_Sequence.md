# Viriatus Murder Sequence — Founding Era

## Purpose

This file locks the private sequence of Viriatus's murder and the public sequence that turns the murder into Regulatus.

It does not create new lore.

It clarifies what the existing files already establish so the Founding Era can be used as a stable base for later eras.

The goal is to separate:

- what actually happened
- what the public was told
- why each person acted
- who benefited
- who was hurt
- how the hidden crime survived as official history

## Status

Canon for the Founding Era.

This file should be updated only if the core founding sequence changes across the history, calendar, or Ventari family files.

## Timeline Lock

The order must stay clear:

> Viriatus conquers under Ventari control  
> -> Ecalus dies before creating the final new order  
> -> House Ventari mourns  
> -> Teresta is away  
> -> Rutare cries, panics, or witnesses enough to know the official story is false  
> -> Viriatus kneels over Ecalus's casket as a grieving son  
> -> Ilunis stabs Viriatus in the back  
> -> Ermerus controls the aftermath  
> -> Servan witnesses are blamed for stealing jewelry during mourning  
> -> Servan witnesses are killed  
> -> the public story becomes noble death in war  
> -> Ermerus declares Regulatus  
> -> Year 0 AVD begins

AVD begins at the public declaration of Regulatus, not at the private moment Viriatus dies.

## Private Truth

Viriatus is murdered inside the family sphere after Ecalus dies.

He is not killed in honorable battle.

He is not killed by enemy soldiers.

He is not killed while trying to seize power.

He is killed while grieving Ecalus as a son.

Ilunis stabs him in the back after Ermerus turns Ilunis's jealousy, maiming, and resentment into family duty.

The private truth is:

> Viriatus was murdered by House Ventari because his living public meaning threatened the blood heirs' control of what came after Ecalus.

## Public Story

The public story becomes:

> Viriatus died a noble death in war.

This story is false.

It is useful because it gives the public a clean grief object.

It removes the family betrayal.

It removes Ilunis's role.

It removes Ermerus's manipulation.

It removes the fact that Viriatus was grieving the same father the siblings claimed to honor.

The public does not receive a murder.

The public receives sacrifice.

## Political Use

Ermerus uses the public story to transform grief into obedience.

Viriatus alive could gather loyalty around himself.

Viriatus dead can gather grief around a story Ermerus controls.

The political use is:

> private murder -> public noble death -> public grief -> doctrine -> Regulatus -> AVD

Regulatus becomes the order that claims to preserve what Viriatus won.

To question Regulatus becomes to dishonor Viriatus.

To obey Regulatus becomes to participate in his legacy.

## Hidden Cause

The murder happens because Viriatus's meaning changes after Ecalus dies.

Before Ecalus's death, Viriatus is useful to House Ventari because his victories prove Ventari discipline.

After Ecalus's death, Viriatus becomes dangerous because people could gather around him as the obvious public figure for the reshaped Rome.

The danger is not that Viriatus leaves House Ventari.

The danger is that his meaning outranks House Ventari's blood heirs.

The family created the myth, but while Viriatus lives the myth can still belong emotionally to him.

Ermerus understands that a living Viriatus can become the center of the new order.

A dead Viriatus can become the foundation of an order Ermerus interprets.

## Ermerus's Role

Ermerus does not need to be the person holding the blade.

His role is interpretation.

He sees Ilunis's wound and gives it a reason that feels noble enough to act on.

Ilunis does not need to tell himself:

> I am jealous.

Ermerus gives him:

> Viriatus threatens our blood.

This changes Ilunis's private humiliation into family duty.

Ermerus's chain is:

> experience: Viriatus is loved, victorious, and publicly adored while not being Ventari blood  
> emotion: Ermerus fears the family's future will belong emotionally to Viriatus  
> reason: if Viriatus lives, the new order may center around him instead of the blood heirs  
> behavior: Ermerus redirects Ilunis's wound into murder and then redirects public grief into doctrine  
> atmosphere: mourning becomes ceremony instead of accusation  
> history: Regulatus begins as controlled interpretation of a hidden family crime

Ermerus's power is not force.

His power is deciding what pain means.

## Ilunis's Role

Ilunis kills Viriatus.

His action grows from his wound, not from simple villainy.

Ilunis was overused because he was strong.

He was maimed at Augunar while defending Ventari lands.

After losing mobility, he loses the proof of worth the family trained him to use.

To Ilunis, Viriatus becomes the living proof that his own sacrifice did not earn him the place beside Ecalus.

His private wound is:

> I gave my body to this family, and Father gave his pride to someone else.

Ermerus turns that wound into duty by focusing on blood.

Ilunis's chain is:

> experience: he is maimed, overused, and replaced emotionally by Viriatus  
> emotion: humiliation, resentment, jealousy, and the need to matter again  
> reason: killing Viriatus can feel like protecting the bloodline instead of acting from envy  
> behavior: he stabs Viriatus in the back while Viriatus kneels over Ecalus's casket  
> atmosphere: force becomes the language through which Ilunis can survive shame  
> history: his line later punishes betrayal while descending from betrayal

The murder gives Ilunis agency again.

It is not battlefield glory.

It is private betrayal.

## Rutare's Role

Rutare does not stop the murder.

He witnesses enough to know the official story is false.

He does not become a rebel.

He survives through procedure, silence, records, and pressure leaking through weakness.

Rutare's relationship to Viriatus makes this harder for him.

Viriatus treated Rutare as a person, not only as heir, weakness, obligation, or embarrassment.

Rutare's chain is:

> experience: he knows Viriatus did not die the way the public story says  
> emotion: guilt, grief, fear, inadequacy, and helplessness  
> reason: direct opposition would expose a strength he does not have  
> behavior: he stays silent publicly and turns pressure into procedure, record, and later confession to Teresta  
> atmosphere: bureaucracy begins as a way to act without thinking  
> history: the same records that help formalize the lie may preserve contradictions inside it

Rutare's silence helps the lie survive.

Rutare's inability to stop recording may also let traces of the truth survive.

## Teresta's Role

Teresta is away during the murder.

That absence matters.

She does not participate in the killing.

She does not participate in the immediate cover-up.

She is not part of the first lie as it is being created.

She learns the truth later through Rutare.

Rutare tells her because she treats him with care and because he needs to release the pressure of what he knows.

Teresta's chain is:

> experience: she learns that Viriatus's death was masked murder  
> emotion: rejection, disgust, grief, and refusal  
> reason: if she stabilizes the family order, she becomes part of the lie  
> behavior: she distances herself from House Ventari instead of supporting the new structure  
> atmosphere: her line begins outside secure family power  
> history: her daughter-line is later rejected and survives through distance, study, and delayed usefulness

Teresta's refusal is not rebellion at first.

It is distance.

Distance is the only refusal she can choose without immediately becoming a rival power.

## Viriatus's Position At Death

Viriatus dies at the point where his private identity and public meaning collide.

Privately, he is grieving Ecalus as a son.

Publicly, he is already becoming the obvious figure for the reshaped Rome.

That collision makes the murder especially useful and especially cruel.

If the public knew Viriatus was murdered while grieving Ecalus, House Ventari's new order would begin as visible betrayal.

If the public believes Viriatus died nobly in war, House Ventari can make the new order feel like preservation.

The hidden truth would create accusation.

The public story creates duty.

## Servan Witnesses

Servan witnesses are used as the false direction for blame.

Ermerus convinces the guards that Servan witnesses stole jewelry during the mourning period.

This accusation does two things:

1. It explains confusion around the mourning period through a lower-class crime.
2. It removes people who could contradict the family-controlled version of events.

The Servans are killed.

This repeats the origin pattern of Viriatus's arrival.

At the arrival, Servans saw the truth first and were erased so House Ventari could control Viriatus's origin.

At the death, Servans again become disposable material so House Ventari can control Viriatus's meaning.

The founding pattern is:

> lower witnesses see too much  
> House Ventari treats their testimony as danger  
> the witnesses are removed  
> the public receives a controlled story

## Why The Public Accepts The Story

The public accepts the story because the emotional conditions already exist.

Old Rome has been framed as weak, corrupt, hungry, exploitative, and unable to protect families.

Viriatus's victories have been framed as proof that Ventari discipline can create order where old Rome failed.

The people already feel fear, hunger, humiliation, resentment, grief, exhaustion, and desire for stability.

When Viriatus dies, Ermerus gives them an interpretation that protects the hope Viriatus represented.

The public reason becomes:

> If Viriatus gave himself so order could survive, then order must not be allowed to scatter after his death.

This is why Regulatus can feel like gratitude instead of domination.

From inside the public story, obedience is not surrender.

It is participation in Viriatus's legacy.

## Regulatus Result

Regulatus is not created by Viriatus.

Regulatus is created from Viriatus's death by Ermerus.

It turns:

- grief into duty
- admiration into obedience
- loss into gratitude
- hierarchy into protection
- discipline into moral worth
- family control into civilization

Regulatus claims to preserve what Viriatus won.

In truth, it preserves House Ventari's control over what Viriatus means.

## Causal Chain

### Private Murder Chain

> Viriatus conquers under Ventari control  
> -> Ecalus begins loving him and later shows that love near death  
> -> Ecalus dies before establishing the final order  
> -> Viriatus's living public meaning threatens the siblings' control  
> -> Ermerus interprets the threat through blood and succession  
> -> Ilunis receives this interpretation through his own maiming and jealousy  
> -> Ilunis kills Viriatus privately  
> -> Rutare cannot stop it and later carries the pressure  
> -> Teresta is absent and later rejects the truth when she learns it

### Public History Chain

> private murder  
> -> Servan witnesses blamed and killed  
> -> public story becomes noble death in war  
> -> public grief is redirected into ceremony  
> -> Ermerus declares Regulatus  
> -> Year 0 AVD begins  
> -> House Ventari gains an order that turns Viriatus's stolen legacy into mass control

## Consistency Notes

- Viriatus does not create Regulatus.
- Ecalus starts the plot but dies before personally creating the final order.
- Ilunis kills Viriatus privately after Ecalus dies.
- Ermerus controls the aftermath and declares Regulatus.
- Rutare witnesses enough to know the official story is false but does not publicly challenge it.
- Teresta is away during the murder and learns the truth later through Rutare.
- AVD begins when Regulatus is publicly declared, not at the exact private moment of death.
- The public story is noble death in war.
- The private truth is murder during family mourning.
