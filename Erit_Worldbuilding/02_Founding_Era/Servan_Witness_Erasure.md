# Servan Witness Erasure — Founding Era

## Purpose

This file locks the role of Servans in the Founding Era.

It explains why Servans matter to Viriatus's origin, why House Ventari removes them, why Ermerus uses them again after Viriatus's murder, and how servant testimony becomes one of the first kinds of truth House Ventari learns to erase.

This file should prevent ambiguity around Servans.

Servans are servants of the house.

They are not a separate people, ethnicity, faction, or rival institution.

Their importance comes from their position inside House Ventari's private world:

- close enough to see what elites hide
- low-status enough to be disbelieved, blamed, punished, or removed
- dependent enough that their testimony can be controlled by the house that employs them

## Definition

Servans are servants of the house.

In the Founding Era, this includes house servants, field servants, and other domestic or estate workers attached to House Ventari.

They are not outside the family structure in the same way rival houses are outside it.

They live and work close to elite rooms, fields, mourning spaces, storage, travel, preparation, and private household movement.

This makes them useful to House Ventari.

It also makes them dangerous.

They can see what the public cannot.

---

## Why Servans Matter

Servans matter because they witness truth before the public story is built.

They do not create the founding crimes.

They do not control the political meaning.

They do not have the status to decide history.

But they are present at the moments House Ventari needs to hide:

1. Viriatus's arrival.
2. Viriatus's death.

At both moments, Servans become dangerous because their testimony would expose that House Ventari's official version is controlled interpretation, not clean truth.

---

## First Erasure — Viriatus's Arrival

### Private Truth

Servans working the fields see a light appear in front of them.

Then a child appears.

They take the child to Ecalus Ventari and tell him he fell from the heavens.

Ecalus does not believe them at first.

He thinks they are mocking him.

Because Servans are beneath House Ventari's authority, their impossible claim sounds to him less like revelation and more like insult.

Ecalus orders guards to punish them.

Viriatus sees the punishment begin and reacts.

He pushes the guards across the room, injuring one badly enough to break his shoulder.

At that moment, Ecalus understands that the Servans may not have been lying.

He also understands that Viriatus responds violently to visible harm against the weak.

### Hidden Cause

The Servans must be removed because they know the true origin before House Ventari has controlled it.

If their version spreads, Viriatus could become:

- divine
- public property
- a child of the gods
- a miracle belonging to the people
- a threat to Rome
- a weapon belonging to the army
- a prize other houses could contest
- someone House Ventari has no natural right to own

That would make him impossible for Ecalus to keep uncontested.

### Ecalus's Reason

Ecalus reasons that Viriatus cannot be controlled through open cruelty.

The guard incident proves that visible punishment may turn the child against him.

So Ecalus separates what Viriatus experiences from what House Ventari does.

To Viriatus, House Ventari becomes shelter.

To the Servans, House Ventari becomes silence.

### Behavior

The Servans disappear quietly.

Viriatus is never told.

House Ventari hides the true origin.

### Public Story

House Ventari does not publicly say:

> We found him.

The public story becomes:

> We formed him.

Viriatus is framed as a child House Ventari trained, taught, disciplined, and shaped.

His victories later become proof that Ventari discipline can create order where old Rome failed.

### Result

The first hidden crime of the Founding Era is not Viriatus's murder.

It is the erasure of the Servans who first brought him to safety.

The founding pattern begins here:

> lower witnesses see too much  
> -> House Ventari treats their testimony as danger  
> -> the witnesses are removed  
> -> the public receives a controlled story

---

## Second Erasure — Viriatus's Death

### Private Truth

After Ecalus dies, House Ventari mourns.

Viriatus kneels over Ecalus's casket as a grieving son.

Ilunis stabs Viriatus in the back after Ermerus turns Ilunis's jealousy, maiming, resentment, and bloodline wound into family duty.

Viriatus does not die in honorable war.

He is murdered privately inside the family sphere.

### Why Servans Are Dangerous Again

Servans are close enough to the household to witness confusion, movement, missing objects, blood, timing, grief, panic, or contradictions around the mourning period.

Even if every Servan does not see the blade itself, their presence threatens the clean version House Ventari needs.

Their testimony could show that Viriatus died during family mourning, not in war.

That would expose:

- Ilunis's betrayal
- Ermerus's manipulation
- Rutare's silence
- House Ventari's private fracture
- the fact that the new order begins with siblings killing the adopted son Ecalus loved

### Ermerus's Reason

Ermerus understands that the public cannot receive the private truth.

If the public learns that Viriatus was murdered while grieving Ecalus, the new order begins as accusation.

If the public believes Viriatus died nobly in war, the new order can begin as duty.

Ermerus needs a false direction for blame.

Servans are useful because their lower status makes the accusation easier to accept.

### Behavior

Ermerus convinces the guards that Servan witnesses stole jewelry during the mourning period.

The accusation does two things:

1. It explains confusion around the mourning period through a lower-status crime.
2. It removes people who could contradict the family-controlled version of events.

The Servan witnesses are killed.

### Public Story

The public story becomes:

> Viriatus died a noble death in war.

The Servan accusation does not become the main heroic story.

It functions as internal cover.

It gives guards and household enforcers a reason to remove witnesses before the public story fully hardens.

### Result

At the arrival, Servans see the truth first and are erased so House Ventari can control Viriatus's origin.

At the death, Servans again become disposable material so House Ventari can control Viriatus's meaning.

## Repeated Founding Pattern

The two Servan erasures mirror each other.

| Moment | What Servans Know | Ventari Threat | Ventari Response | Public Result |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Viriatus arrives | A light appeared and a child came from it | Viriatus could become divine, public, contested, or unownable | Ecalus removes the Servans quietly | House Ventari claims it formed him |
| Viriatus dies | The death happened during family mourning, not war | The new order could begin as visible betrayal | Ermerus blames Servans for jewelry theft and removes them | Viriatus becomes noble war-dead |

This pattern matters because House Ventari learns that history can be built by removing people whose position gives them access to truth but not protection.

---

## Public And Private Truth

### Private Truth

Servans are the first witnesses of Viriatus's true arrival.

Servan witnesses later threaten the concealment of Viriatus's true death.

In both cases, House Ventari removes them because their testimony would weaken family control over meaning.

### Public Story

The public is not told that Servans saw Viriatus fall from the heavens.

The public is not told that Servan witnesses were removed to protect House Ventari after Viriatus's murder.

The public receives House Ventari's controlled version:

- Viriatus was formed by Ventari discipline.
- Viriatus died nobly in war.
- Regulatus preserves what he won.

### Political Use

The erasure of Servans makes controlled history possible.

House Ventari benefits because it can claim authorship over Viriatus in life and ownership over his meaning in death.

Servans are hurt because their closeness to truth gives them no protection.

Viriatus is hurt because the first people who brought him to safety are erased without his knowledge.

The public is hurt because the grief used to create Regulatus is built on hidden murder and witness removal.

---

## Why This Survives Into History

Servan erasure survives because it becomes useful at more than one level.

### Household Level

Inside House Ventari, servants learn that proximity to truth is dangerous.

Silence becomes survival.

### Family Level

The Ventari siblings learn that inconvenient witnesses can be turned into explanations.

Servans can absorb blame that cannot be allowed to touch blood heirs.

### Political Level

The public receives a cleaner story because the people who could complicate it are removed before the story spreads.

### Historical Level

Later history remembers the public meaning, not the erased testimony.

The official version survives because the private witnesses do not.

## Causal Chain

### Arrival Chain

> experience: Servans witness the light and bring Viriatus to Ecalus  
> -> emotion: Ecalus feels insult, then shock, then strategic fear  
> -> reason: if their testimony spreads, Viriatus becomes impossible for House Ventari to control  
> -> behavior: Ecalus removes the Servans quietly and hides the origin  
> -> atmosphere: House Ventari appears protective while privately erasing truth  
> -> history: Viriatus becomes public proof of Ventari discipline instead of a child fallen from the heavens

### Death Chain

> experience: Viriatus is murdered privately during family mourning  
> -> emotion: Ermerus feels urgency to control the aftermath  
> -> reason: if witnesses survive, the public story can collapse into accusation  
> -> behavior: Ermerus blames Servan witnesses for jewelry theft and has them killed  
> -> atmosphere: mourning becomes ceremony instead of investigation  
> -> history: Viriatus becomes noble war-dead, Regulatus is declared, and AVD begins
