# Faction Layer Overview

## Purpose

This file organizes the current faction and social layers for later NPC and quest design.

It does not create quests yet.

Its purpose is to keep track of:

- which layer a faction belongs to
- what the faction publicly claims
- what it privately does
- what words, phrases, and pressures can be reused later in quest design

Future quests should come from these layers only after the world systems are stable.

## Core Layer Rule

A faction is not only a team.

A faction is a pressure system.

For later quest design, every faction should be read through:

```txt
experience -> emotion -> reason -> behavior -> atmosphere -> history
```

For NPCs, public alignment and private belief may differ.

A person may publicly belong to Regulatus while privately sympathizing with rebels, depending on where they live, who watches them, and what they need to survive.

# Layer One — Public Alignment

## Regulatus

### Public Meaning

Regulatus is the accepted public order.

Inside Vys, everyone is Regulatus in public.

This does not mean everyone believes in Regulatus privately. It means the city has become structured so that appearing outside Regulatus is dangerous.

### Main Pressure

Public loyalty must be performed.

People are expected to attend rituals, respect the statue of Viriatus, use approved language, avoid visible doubt, and show obedience in public spaces.

### Enforcement

The Scutis make this difficult to hide from.

As the protective division of the Virtuasti, the Scutis guard streets, civic buildings, gates, rituals, statue ceremonies, government offices, and public gatherings.

Over time, protection becomes visibility.

They notice who attends, who hesitates, who avoids oaths, who receives visitors, who moves at strange hours, and who appears too detached from public loyalty.

### Quest Use Later

Regulatus creates quests about:

- public loyalty
- identity checks
- record pressure
- hidden doubt
- ritual attendance
- surveillance through protection
- NPCs saying one thing and believing another

### Key Words

order, duty, public loyalty, records, obedience, visibility, approval, ceremony, oath, suspicion, correction

---

## Rebel

### Public Meaning

Rebels are treated by Regulatus as disorder, threat, corruption, and opposition.

### Private Function

The rebellion begins as city resistance, then becomes independent of cities after conquest and pressure make local resistance unsafe.

Later, rebels are forced toward the desert and align with villages to survive.

### Main Pressure

The rebels are united by opposition to Regulatus, but they are poor in resources.

They survive through scarcity, village alliances, mobility, safehouses, desert routes, hidden wells, stolen supplies, and trust.

### Quest Use Later

Rebels create quests about:

- scarce resources
- desert survival
- village alliances
- infiltration
- sabotage
- smuggling
- moral compromise
- whether resistance helps or endangers civilians

### Key Words

scarcity, resistance, desert, village, hidden wells, sabotage, deserters, safehouses, survival, hunger, stolen supplies, purpose

---

## Neutral

### Public Meaning

Neutral groups are not openly loyal to Regulatus and not openly rebel.

Neutrality is not innocence.

It is often a survival strategy.

### Main Example

Arumis during Era Two and early Era Three.

Arumis keeps trade with Vys to avoid confrontation while building universities, metallurgy, mining knowledge, port wealth, and future power.

Its trade helps Vys fight Lucrest, which creates a moral wound.

### Quest Use Later

Neutrality creates quests about:

- trade with enemies
- survival through compromise
- guilt from indirect harm
- merchants and academics avoiding open alignment
- factions using neutral spaces
- the cost of staying useful

### Key Words

trade, compromise, survival, useful, delay, guilt, port, university, metallurgy, neutrality, hidden growth

# Regulatus-Aligned Factions

### Layer

Regulatus.

### Public Face

Government, civic order, law, records, taxation, official administration, public ritual.

### Private Function

Vys Authority turns Regulatus from declaration into daily life.

It governs through records, offices, public ceremony, identity recognition, taxes, permissions, and civic procedure.

### Quest Use Later

- identity recognition
- permits
- taxes
- legal status
- bureaucracy
- public obedience
- corrupt officials

### Key Words

office, seal, record, permit, census, approval, recognition, tax, registry, civic duty

## Virtuasti

### Layer

Regulatus-aligned, but not obedient to normal government.

### Public Face

The pride of the military.

The living continuation of Viriatus's public purpose.

### Private Function

The Virtuasti enforce Regulatus when ordinary government, courts, or charity are considered too slow or too weak.

They do not follow ordinary civic law.

They are loyal to Regulatus, but not obedient to the government that claims to administer it.

They act with free reign because they are too powerful and too useful to restrain.

### Origin

Varric Ilunis returns from pilgrimage with the Banner of Viriatus and creates the Virtuasti.

The leadership plays ignorant when useful. They know Viriatus is more powerful as a public image than Ermerus ever could be.

They use Viriatus's name because anyone would rather join the conqueror's way than admit they serve a political order born from betrayal.

### Virtus

Virtus means valor, disciplined strength, honor, pride, and duty.

The Virtuasti are not founded as a religious order. Their power comes from purpose.

### Divisions

#### Spatis

Offensive force used to pursue, break, and abolish rebel groups.

#### Scutis

Protective force assigned to cities, routes, civic centers, rituals, and Regulatus-aligned populations.

In Vys, Scutis protection becomes public visibility.

#### Umbris

Assassins and spies used for infiltration, sabotage, intelligence, disappearance, and hidden threat removal.

### Oath

"Valor my sword. Pride my shield. Death my freedom."

This oath is used by Virtuasti soldiers before combat, especially among the Spatis.

It teaches that a soldier of Virtus should act with courage, defend themselves through pride, and treat death as release from fear rather than failure.

### Quest Use Later

Virtuasti create quests about:

- military recruitment
- rank trials
- rebel suppression
- city protection becoming surveillance
- assassination orders
- bypassing law
- loyalty to Regulatus versus obedience to government
- corruption control
- ruthless honor

### Key Words

Virtus, valor, pride, banner, Viriatus, honor, purpose, abolition, oath, free reign, correction, zeal, force

## Lux Aeterna

### Layer

Regulatus-aligned soft control.

### Public Face

Mercy, food, shelter, basic care, burial rites, orphan support, moral guidance, help for the poor.

### Private Function

Lux Aeterna spreads Ventari influence through aid.

It records the poor, creates dependency, builds gratitude, and reaches places soldiers and courts cannot easily enter.

### Bloodline Root

Founded by a woman from Rutare's line during the Consolidation And Industrial Rise Era.

### Saying

"The path of light asks for nothing."

Publicly, this means mercy is free.

Privately, it hides the way Lux Aeterna spreads control through visibility, records, gratitude, dependency, and moral debt.

The order does not need to demand loyalty immediately.

It only needs people to step into the light.

### Military Branch

As the Virtuasti grow in prestige, Lux Aeterna creates its own military branch under the justification of purification.

It claims poor lands, damaged cities, and rebel-influenced communities must be protected, cleansed, and restored.

Violence is presented as mercy defended by force.

### Relationship With Virtuasti

The tension is not symmetrical.

The Virtuasti do not acknowledge Lux Aeterna as a true rival.

They see themselves as the pride of the military and the force that enforces Regulatus.

Lux cares more about the comparison because popularity among the poor is not the same as military prestige.

### Quest Use Later

Lux Aeterna creates quests about:

- food and shelter
- orphan care
- charity with hidden records
- moral debt
- purification campaigns
- aid used as surveillance
- poor NPCs choosing between help and freedom

### Key Words

light, mercy, bread, shelter, record, gratitude, dependency, purity, cleansing, guidance, poor, debt, care

## Devaar Authority

### Layer

Emerging Regulatus-aligned body power during Era Four.

### Public Face

Medicine, preservation, body repair, survival, external augmentation.

### Private Function

Devaar rises because it offers survival.

It does not take over first through open conquest. It takes over by making people dependent on augmentation, treatment, prosthetics, and body repair.

### Era Four Augmentation Level

Era Four augmentation begins outside the body.

Examples:

- metal arms
- reinforced hands
- enhanced legs
- support frames
- combat prosthetics
- industrial limb replacements
- military-grade braces

Internal replacement belongs later, closer to Erit's era.

### Quest Use Later

Devaar creates quests about:

- treatment access
- body debt
- military upgrades
- worker augmentation
- prosthetic dependency
- medical contracts
- who deserves repair
- survival as control

### Key Words

augmentation, preservation, prosthetic, treatment, body, repair, dependency, clinic, contract, survival, replacement, access

# Rebel And Opposition Layers

## Lucrest Rebellion

### Layer

Rebel.

### Public Face

Resistance against Regulatus.

### Private Function

Lucrest becomes the rebel heart because it holds fertile land, food production, rivers, water leverage, mountain-adjacent terrain, and Ermerus's children after his death.

### Historical Irony

Ermerus creates Regulatus through blood, betrayal, and public meaning.

After his death, his own children gather in Lucrest, where rebellion against Regulatus becomes strongest.

### Quest Use Later

Lucrest creates quests about:

- rebel legitimacy
- water and food leverage
- mountain routes
- siege memory
- bloodline irony
- post-conquest resentment

### Key Words

Lucrest, river, food, mountain, rebel heart, rejected blood, siege, water, resistance, legitimacy

Rebel.

- water scarcity

## Northern Cities

### Layer

Regional opposition / future pressure layer.

### Public Face

Oath-bound northern cities with gods, spirits, clan honor, warriors, iron, ships, and gladiator tradition.

### Faith And Blessing Rites

Northern rites are not fireball magic or visible spellcasting.

They are blessings used to ask gods, ancestors, land, storms, beasts, rivers, mountains, and the remembered dead for strength, fortitude, courage, endurance, and protection.

### Saying

"Salt my wounds. Sharpen my axe."

This is a northern battle blessing spoken before combat, hardship, or vengeance.

It asks for pain to become fortitude and suffering to become readiness.

### Historical Role

The northern cities become major sources of gladiators during the Consolidation And Industrial Rise Era.

Over time, this creates pressure against Ventari because northern strength is turned into southern spectacle, recruitment, and political measurement.

The North starts a war during Era Three and the war ends by the end of that era.

Much later, Valkare annihilates what the northern pressure becomes.

### Quest Use Later

Northern cities create quests about:

- clan oaths
- blessings before battle
- gladiator origins
- northern resentment
- warrior honor
- iron supply
- anti-Ventari pressure

### Key Words

north, oath, clan, axe, salt, wound, blessing, gods, ancestors, fortitude, iron, storm, warrior, clan hall

# Side Factions

## Gladiator Houses

### Layer

Side faction / neutral-adjacent social power.

### Public Face

Arenas, champions, family prestige, entertainment, controlled violence.

### Private Function

Gladiator Houses let families measure strength without open war.

They also become useful to every major faction.

### Main Sources Of Gladiators

Most gladiators come from the northern cities.

Lucrest contributes captured rebels, political prisoners, and disgraced fighters.

Arumis contributes dock workers, miners, debtors, and sponsored fighters from corrupt trade networks.

### Rebel Use

Rebels use Gladiator Houses to infiltrate Regulatus-controlled spaces.

A gladiator can enter arenas, noble patron rooms, weapon stores, healer tents, betting houses, festivals, and transport routes without being treated as a political threat.

Messages can move through betting tokens, bandages, weapon shipments, sponsor gifts, crowd chants, and arena workers.

### Starting Class

Gladiator can become a starting class later.

The class begins inside a side faction that all major powers use.

The core pressure:

The body has value because other people can use it.

### Quest Use Later

Gladiator Houses create quests about:

- arena reputation
- sponsorship
- fighter debt
- rebel infiltration
- Virtuasti scouting
- Lux healing wounded fighters
- noble family prestige
- escaping the arena system

### Key Words

arena, champion, sponsor, wager, crowd, blood, fame, body, fighter, northern, contract, spectacle, infiltration

# Faction Design Notes For Later Quests

## Public Layer Versus Private Belief

Do not assume public alignment equals inner loyalty.

Examples:

- A Vys citizen publicly performs Regulatus but privately fears Scutis.
- An Arumis merchant trades with Vys while quietly resenting how that trade fuels war.
- A gladiator fights for a noble sponsor while carrying rebel messages.
- A poor family accepts Lux Aeterna food while hating the records that come with it.
- A Virtuasti soldier believes in Virtus more than in the government.

## Same Action, Different Meaning

Use faction pressure to change how actions are interpreted.

Examples:

- Giving food may be charity to Lux, bribery to Vys Authority, weakness to Virtuasti, survival to Rebels, and debt to the poor.
- Wearing a Viriatus symbol may signal loyalty in Vys, recruitment value to Virtuasti, danger to Rebels, and spectacle value in the arena.
- Refusing a record may mean privacy to a citizen, guilt to Vys Authority, rebellion to Scutis, and protection to a desert village.

## Future Quest Rule

Quests should come from conflict between at least three pressures.

Example:

```txt
world pressure -> character need -> obstacle -> approaches -> consequence
```

A good quest should not only ask what the player does.

It should ask what that action means to each faction watching it.
