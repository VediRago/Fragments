# Timeline And Era Summaries — Erit World

## Purpose

This file is the readable historical timeline for Erit's world.

The calendar file explains how the calendar and era structure were created. This file keeps the events and era summaries in one place so later eras can be built from the consequences of earlier ones.

## Calendar Anchor

Erit's main historical calendar is AVD.

AVD means:

> After Viriatus's Death

Year 0 AVD does not begin at the private moment Viriatus dies.

Year 0 AVD begins when Ermerus publicly declares Regulatus and successfully defines what Viriatus's death means.

The private truth is murder. The public story is noble death in war. The calendar begins when the official story wins.

## Era Four — Game World / Preservation And Augmentation Era

Era Four runs from roughly **1000 AVD to 3000 AVD**.

Era Four is the main playable world-state before Erit's far-future era, but the game world does not begin at the start of the era. It begins late in Era Four, around **2600 AVD**, during the Dark Time.

### Era Four Opening — Vys Metropolis

Era Four begins with Vys becoming a metropolis.

The city grows far beyond the scale of the other major cities because Era Three consolidated the resources of Vys, Lucrest, and Arumis. Vys holds government and public alignment. Lucrest feeds it through fertile lands, rivers, and water systems. Arumis supplies medicine, knowledge, metallurgy, coal, iron, and port trade.

This growth changes the city’s body.

The pearl-like structures of old Vys continue giving way to metal, concrete, administrative towers, dense districts, early engines, ration offices, Scutis patrol routes, Lux aid halls, and Virtuasti staging grounds.

Vys becomes larger, heavier, and harder to escape.

Era Four does not begin with collapse.

It begins with overgrowth.

### Scutis Expansion

At the beginning of Era Four, Vys’s growth creates overpopulation, poverty pockets, smuggling, corruption, black markets, and crime.

The old civic systems cannot control the size of the metropolis, and public fear gives the Virtuasti an opening.

The Scutis step in under the justification of restoring order.

Their presence begins as protection, but protection becomes visibility. They guard streets, stations, ration lines, civic centers, hospitals, Lux halls, markets, ports, and transport routes. They watch movement, absence, suspicious behavior, illegal trade, and public loyalty.

This expansion does not stay inside Vys.

From this point onward, the Scutis are present in all major cities.

The official reason is safety.

The lasting result is that Regulatus gains a visible protective force embedded into daily life everywhere.

### Modern Industrial Stage

By Era Four, the world has moved into a modern industrial stage.

Guns, planes, engines, cars, metal structures, concrete, telecommunications, and large city infrastructure exist before cybernetics become common.

This matters because cybernetics do not appear in a medieval world. They appear after centuries of industrial growth, war, medicine, metallurgy, transport systems, body injury, and labor demand.

### Two Layers Of Cybernetics

Cybernetics develop in two layers.

#### Layer One — External Function

Layer One begins around **2000 AVD**.

This layer is external: metal arms, reinforced hands, enhanced legs, support frames, combat prosthetics, industrial limb replacements, and military braces.

These technologies grow from Arumis metallurgy, engineering, medicine, and the needs of labor, war, gladiator houses, wounded soldiers, and Virtuasti candidates.

Layer One restores function.

#### Layer Two — Devaar Dependency

Layer Two begins around **2330 AVD** with Devaar.

Devaar does not simply improve external limbs. It turns cybernetics into medical infrastructure: clinics, treatment contracts, repair dependency, military enhancement programs, preservation systems, standardized body parts, and controlled access to bodily function.

Layer Two controls survival.

The difference matters.

The first layer helps people recover function.

The second layer makes survival pass through Devaar.

### Solar Flare Famine — Around 2500 AVD

The solar flare happens late in Era Four, around **2500 AVD**, after external cybernetics and augmentation have already entered society.

This timing matters.

The flare does not create Devaar’s rise from nothing. Devaar is already powerful through medicine, preservation, external augmentation, metal limbs, enhanced legs, braces, combat prosthetics, and treatment systems.

The flare makes that power unavoidable.

When the surface is blasted and crops fail, the main cities enter famine, rationing, and stagnation. Vys is a metropolis by then, but its size becomes weakness. It depends on food routes, water systems, medicine, industrial labor, and controlled supply chains.

The Dark Time begins after the flare.

### Game World Start — The Dark Time

The playable game world begins during the Dark Time, late in Era Four, around **2600 AVD**.

By this period, the old institutions still exist, but they are strained.

Vys tries to hold order. Devaar rises because preservation and external augmentation become survival tools. Lux Aeterna spreads aid and dependency. The Virtuasti enforce Regulatus with greater autonomy. The Novacula attack roads. The North pressures the seas. Arumis becomes vital because medicine and knowledge can keep people alive.

This is the playable moment because the systems are mature, but not settled.

The player enters a world where every faction can claim it is protecting people, and every form of protection creates another chain of control.

### Desert Motorcades And Refineries

By Era Four, desert villages and trade routes develop into motorcade culture.

The old caravan life does not disappear. It changes shape.

Desert people move through armored cars, bikes, patched trucks, fuel carts, water tank vehicles, mobile markets, gambling rigs, and weapon-mounted convoys. They remain traders, gamblers, smugglers, and negotiators, but engines let them move faster than Regulatus patrols expect.

By this era, desert villages and motorcade groups also control oil refineries across the sands.

This changes their role.

The desert is no longer only a place where the Novacula hide, trade routes twist, and Regulatus patrols disappear. It becomes a resource region that Vys and the wider Regulatus system cannot ignore.

Engines, cars, planes, military vehicles, industrial machines, and later external cybernetic production all depend on fuel and refinery access.

The desert people understand this.

They value freedom more than comfort, and oil gives that freedom teeth. If Regulatus controls the refineries, the sands become mapped, taxed, patrolled, and owned. If the desert controls them, Vys has to bargain or bleed.

This creates repeated wars with Regulatus.

Desert motorcades defend refineries, sabotage routes, ambush convoys, sell false maps, strip vehicles, and help the Novacula make Regulatus suffer whenever it tries to take the sands.

They remain warm, charming, trade-minded, and fond of anything shiny.

But by Era Four, their hospitality has teeth.

### Vys-North Alliance Against The Dunes

During Era Four, Vys creates an alliance with the North to pressure the desert.

The desert has become too important to ignore. Its villages and motorcades control oil refineries across the sands, and Novacula-linked groups use the routes to attack Regulatus movement. Fuel, roads, and supply lines become impossible to secure through city soldiers alone.

Vys turns to the North.

The alliance is practical, not affectionate. The North has already resisted Ventari influence before, but northern fighters understand endurance, harsh terrain, heavy armor, and survival under punishing conditions better than most city forces. Vys needs that kind of strength in the dunes.

The North accepts because the alliance gives it leverage: access to fuel, modern weapons, trade, recognition, and a chance to prove that northern strength still matters in the new age.

Together, Vys and the North pressure the desert refineries and motorcade routes.

This creates a brutal triangle in the game-world period:

Vys wants fuel and order.

The North wants leverage and proof.

The desert wants freedom and control over its own lands.

### North In Era Four

By Era Four, northern fighters still keep their blessing rites, but their weapons have changed.

They still speak old incantations before battle, including:

> Salt my wounds. Sharpen my axe.

The North does not see modern weapons as a contradiction. A weapon is only another tool for the oath.

Northern fighters carry machine guns, heavy armor, reinforced cold-weather gear, and clan-marked weapons. Their armor is famously difficult for outsiders to use. It is built around northern conditioning: heavy plates, cold-weather reinforcement, brutal weight distribution, and the expectation that the wearer has been trained from youth to endure discomfort, hunger, cold, and strain.

To the North, this proves their old belief: northern life produces stronger people than southern comfort.

To outsiders, it makes northern fighters look like relics wearing the future.

## Era Five — Erit Era

Era Five begins after roughly **3000 AVD**.

Status: not built yet.

This is the far-future, cyberpunk-like inherited world of Erit's branch.

By this era, House Devaar has major power through medicine, preservation, chips, augmentation, and treatment access.

The old Ventari wounds survive through daily systems: identity, access, law, status, dependency, augmentation, surveillance, and control.

Core transformation:

> old wounds -> inherited systems -> cyberpunk pressure -> lived identity control
