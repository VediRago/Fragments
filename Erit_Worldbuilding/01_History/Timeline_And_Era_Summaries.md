# Timeline And Era Summaries — Erit World

## Purpose

This file is the readable historical timeline for Erit's world.

The calendar file explains how the calendar and era structure were created. This file keeps the events and era summaries in one place so later eras can be built from the consequences of earlier ones.

## Calendar Anchor

Erit's main historical calendar is AVD.

AVD means:

> After Viriatus's Death

Year 0 AVD does not begin at the private moment Viriatus dies.

Year 0 AVD begins when Ermerus publicly declares Regulatus and successfully defines what Viriatus's death means.

The private truth is murder. The public story is noble death in war. The calendar begins when the official story wins.

---

## Before AVD — Old Rome And House Ventari

Before Regulatus, Rome is shaped by sixteen elite houses. House Ventari is one of them, but it is not the wealthiest. That position creates pressure for Ecalus Ventari: his house has influence, but influence still depends on negotiation, marriage, military reputation, civic authority, and public meaning.

Ecalus wants House Ventari to stop competing for power and begin defining it.

---

## Founding Era — From Viriatus's Arrival To Year 0 AVD

### Viriatus Appears

Servans working under House Ventari see a light appear in front of them. Then a child appears.

Servans are servants of the house, not a separate people, ethnicity, faction, or rival institution. Their position matters because they are close enough to see what elites hide, but low-status enough to be disbelieved, blamed, punished, or removed.

They bring the child to Ecalus and say he fell from the heavens. Ecalus first thinks they are mocking him and orders guards to punish them.

Viriatus sees the punishment begin and reacts. He pushes the guards across the room, and one guard hits the wall hard enough to break his shoulder.

Ecalus immediately understands that Viriatus is not normal, that the Servans may have told the truth, and that visible harm can awaken Viriatus's protective violence.

### The First Erasure

The Servans become dangerous because they know the true origin. If their version spreads, Viriatus could become divine, public, contested, claimed by rival houses, or impossible for House Ventari to keep uncontested.

Ecalus removes the Servan witnesses quietly. Viriatus is never told.

This creates the first founding pattern of Era One: lower witnesses see too much, House Ventari treats their testimony as danger, the witnesses are removed, and the public receives a controlled story.

### The Public Origin

House Ventari does not publicly say Viriatus fell from the heavens.

House Ventari does not publicly say:

> We found him.

The public story becomes:

> We formed him.

Publicly, Viriatus is framed as a child House Ventari trained, taught, disciplined, and shaped. His greatness becomes proof of Ventari discipline.

### Ecalus Shapes Viriatus

Ecalus gives Viriatus shelter, food, language, education, discipline, family structure, a name, purpose, and a place in the world.

To Viriatus, House Ventari feels like rescue. To Ecalus, this care is also control.

Ecalus does not control Viriatus mainly through fear. He controls him by becoming the source of safety, meaning, and family.

### Viriatus Becomes Proof

Old Rome is weak, divided, hungry, corrupt, and unable to protect families. House Ventari uses public displeasure with the old order to justify replacement.

Viriatus's victories make Ventari discipline look historically proven. The public message becomes that old Rome failed, House Ventari formed Viriatus, Viriatus succeeded, and therefore Ventari discipline should replace the old order.

Viriatus conquers under Ventari control. Ecalus originally dreams of reshaping Rome, but Viriatus delivers Rome and then conquers beyond it. Rome becomes too small to contain what Viriatus proves possible.

### Usefulness Becomes Love

Ecalus does not love Viriatus first. First, he needs him.

Viriatus gives House Ventari what wealth has not given it: inevitability, conquest, public awe, proof of discipline, defeat of the old order, expansion beyond Rome, and a future larger than the sixteen houses.

Every victory makes Viriatus harder for Ecalus to see as only an asset. Viriatus delivers everything Ecalus wanted, then delivers more than Ecalus knew how to imagine.

That is how usefulness becomes love.

Ecalus's love begins during the conquest years. It becomes visible near death. That difference matters.

### The Ventari Children

Viriatus wounds the Ventari children because he becomes both emotionally and historically impossible to compete with.

Ermerus sees Viriatus as proof that blood is not enough. Viriatus threatens him privately by receiving Ecalus's love and publicly by becoming the obvious figure for the reshaped Rome after Ecalus dies.

Rutare is the protected heir, but protection makes him feel weak and useless. Viriatus makes him feel smaller, but also treats him as a person. This creates attachment, guilt, fear, and paralysis.

Ilunis expects usefulness to earn place. Viriatus receives the pride, public admiration, and historical meaning that Ilunis's sacrifice does not. Ermerus later turns Ilunis's humiliation into family duty.

Teresta is away when Viriatus dies. She learns the truth later through Rutare. Her refusal begins as distance, not open rebellion, and her line is later pushed outside secure Ventari power.

### Ecalus Dies

Ecalus dies before creating the final new order.

His death removes the one person whose authority held the contradiction together. Before Ecalus dies, Viriatus is useful to House Ventari because his victories prove Ventari discipline. After Ecalus dies, Viriatus becomes dangerous because people could gather around him as the living center of the reshaped Rome.

The danger is not that Viriatus leaves House Ventari. The danger is that his meaning outranks the blood heirs.

### Viriatus Is Murdered

During family mourning, Viriatus kneels over Ecalus's casket as a grieving son.

Teresta is away. Rutare witnesses enough to know the official story is false. Ermerus redirects Ilunis's wound through blood and family duty.

Ilunis stabs Viriatus in the back.

The private truth is that Viriatus is murdered by House Ventari because his living public meaning threatens the blood heirs' control of what comes after Ecalus.

### The Second Erasure

Servan witnesses are blamed for stealing jewelry during mourning. This accusation explains confusion around the mourning period through a lower-status crime and removes people who could contradict the family-controlled version of events.

Even if every Servan does not see the blade itself, their presence threatens the clean version House Ventari needs. Their testimony could show that Viriatus died during family mourning, not in war.

The Servans are killed.

This repeats the origin pattern. At Viriatus's arrival, Servans see the truth first and are erased so House Ventari can control his origin. At Viriatus's death, Servans again become disposable so House Ventari can control his meaning.

### Regulatus Is Declared

Ermerus turns grief into doctrine.

Viriatus alive could gather loyalty around himself. Viriatus dead can gather grief around a story Ermerus controls.

The political chain is:

> private murder  
> -> public noble death  
> -> public grief  
> -> doctrine  
> -> Regulatus  
> -> AVD

Regulatus is not created by Viriatus. Regulatus is created from Viriatus's death by Ermerus.

To question Regulatus becomes to dishonor Viriatus. To obey Regulatus becomes to participate in his legacy.

### Year 0 AVD

Year 0 AVD marks the moment House Ventari successfully controls the public meaning of Viriatus's death.

It is not the year truth happens.

It is the year the official story wins.

## Era Two — Fracture Era

Status: not built yet.

Era Two does not need to invent a new wound. It inherits the wound Era One hides.

Regulatus survives. The public story survives. The family structure does not remain stable.

Ermerus creates the first era by controlling Viriatus's death and establishing Regulatus, but his own bloodline later fractures through bastard or disputed children. Rutare and Ilunis descendants use legitimacy, blood purity, and protection of order to take power away from Ermerus descendants.

Era Two begins from this irony:

> Ermerus builds an order by controlling Viriatus's meaning through blood, but his own bloodline becomes the weakness that lets the next fracture begin.
