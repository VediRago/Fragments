# NPC System Relationship Model

## Status

Test model.

This file defines an early vocabulary for how NPCs relate to large systems such as Regulatus.

These terms are not final categories. They are working labels to help build NPC behavior, dialogue, atmosphere, and quest pressure without making everyone feel the same.

The model should be tested through future cities, factions, quests, and character examples. If the labels flatten characters or stop helping, they should change.

---

## Purpose

NPCs should not all respond to Regulatus in the same way.

Regulatus is a civilization-wide system, but different people relate to it differently depending on experience, emotion, reason, group pressure, location, and personal history.

This file does not define what Regulatus is.

It defines how a character may relate to it.

The useful question is not only:

> Does this NPC follow Regulatus?

The better question is:

> What relationship does this NPC have with the system?

---

## Core NPC Stack

A future NPC can be built through layered pressure:

> System Relationship  
> Location Pressure  
> Group Pressure  
> Personal State

These layers should all follow the same deeper worldbuilding logic:

> experience -> emotion -> reason -> behavior -> atmosphere -> quest logic

This makes the model fractal.

The world uses the method.

Cities use the method.

Groups use the method.

NPCs use the method.

Quests emerge from the combined pressure.

---

## Layer 1 — System Relationship

System Relationship describes how an NPC relates to a large belief system or social order.

In Erit's world, the main example is Regulatus.

The relationship is not always belief.

Some characters believe it.

Some reject it.

Some are damaged by it.

Some do not understand it.

Some perform belief because the performance gives them power.

This layer should answer:

> How does this character relate to the system that shaped the world around them?

---

## Working Relationship Types

### Believer

A believer accepts the public meaning of the system.

They do not think they are controlled.

They believe the system gives life structure, safety, dignity, and purpose.

A Regulatus believer may think:

> Order protects us.  
> Gratitude proves maturity.  
> My place gives my life meaning.  
> The system is not perfect, but chaos is worse.

Believers should not sound stupid.

They should often sound reasonable from inside their inherited worldview.

Possible behaviors:

- defends hierarchy sincerely
- sees records as protection
- treats obedience as maturity
- distrusts rebels as reckless or ungrateful
- feels pride in social role
- may show kindness while still enforcing harmful rules

---

### Rejector

A rejector sees the system as control, brainwashing, theft, or oppression.

They may be rebels, exiles, forbidden historians, unregistered people, or citizens who were pushed too far.

A Regulatus rejector may think:

> They taught us pride so we would not notice the chains.  
> They call obedience maturity because fear sounds weaker.  
> The system survives by making people grateful for what was taken from them.

Rejectors are not automatically good.

Rejecting the system does not make someone kind, honest, or safe.

Possible behaviors:

- distrusts official language
- reacts badly to loyalist phrases
- avoids records and institutions
- may exploit others in the name of survival
- may see compromise as betrayal
- may help the player only if trust is earned

---

### Damaged Believer

A damaged believer was shaped by the system and still partly speaks its language, but personal experience has cracked their faith.

They may still want to believe.

They may still fear life without the system.

But something happened that made the public meaning feel incomplete or false.

A damaged believer may think:

> Regulatus protected my family once.  
> Regulatus also abandoned my child.  
> If I stop believing, what was all my obedience for?

This type is useful because many people do not change from belief to rebellion instantly.

They live in contradiction.

Possible behaviors:

- repeats official phrases, then hesitates
- bends rules quietly while defending the system publicly
- fears rebels but understands some of their anger
- feels shame when disobeying
- may help the player if the action can be justified as protecting the whole

---

### Outsider

An outsider does not fully share the system's emotional language.

They may be foreign, isolated, newly displaced, raised outside Empiran control, or part of a culture where Regulatus never became natural.

They may see things clearly that insiders normalize.

They may also misunderstand why citizens act the way they do.

An outsider may think:

> Why are they proud of asking permission?  
> Why do they thank the people who restrict them?  
> Why does everyone lower their voice before saying ordinary things?

Outsiders should not automatically be wiser.

They may miss context, underestimate danger, or offend people by treating sacred social habits as obvious control.

Possible behaviors:

- asks blunt questions
- misreads polite obedience as weakness
- fails to understand local risks
- sees contradictions insiders ignore
- may become a bridge for the player to understand the system

---

### Performer / Manipulator

A performer or manipulator knows the public meaning is constructed, but plays along because the performance maintains power, safety, status, or influence.

House Ventari is the strongest example.

They know the reason behind the word.

They play along as if they do not.

This mirrors the structural pattern of Phil and Abby's private language, but with the moral direction inverted.

Phil and Abby:

> surface meaning: sarcasm, correction, joke  
> hidden meaning: affection, ritual, intimacy

House Ventari:

> surface meaning: duty, pride, inherited order  
> hidden meaning: control, theft, manipulation, power preservation

A Ventari performer may publicly say:

> Regulatus protects the whole.

But privately understand:

> Regulatus keeps people proud enough not to ask who benefits.

Performers are not always cartoon villains.

Some may believe the lie is necessary.

Some may fear that without the performance, society collapses.

Some may enjoy the power.

Some may be trapped by the role they inherited.

Possible behaviors:

- speaks official language perfectly
- performs humility while holding power
- redirects uncomfortable truth into noble phrasing
- treats public belief as a tool
- fears exposure more than contradiction
- may respect clever manipulation more than honest resistance

## Layer 2 — Location Pressure

Location Pressure describes what a place makes people fear, value, hide, or repeat.

Examples:

- a city under Ilunis military presence
- a clinic controlled by Teresta medical systems
- a wealthy district watched by Rutare records
- a rebel-held ruin where trust is scarce
- a border settlement afraid of dangerous wildlife
- a district preparing for dynastic inspection

Location Pressure should answer:

> What does this place make people do even before the player speaks?

---

## Layer 3 — Group Pressure

Group Pressure describes how faction, class, job, family role, or social category shapes the NPC.

Examples:

- Ilunis guard
- Rutare clerk
- Teresta doctor
- Ventari noble
- rebel courier
- unregistered worker
- Servan-descended laborer
- clinic patient
- gladiator contractor

Group Pressure should answer:

> What does this person's role require them to protect, fear, prove, or hide?

---

## Layer 4 — Personal State

Personal State is the individual layer.

It includes personal wound, memory, fear, loyalty, habit, debt, hope, shame, and trust threshold.

Two NPCs with the same system relationship, location pressure, and group pressure can still behave differently because of personal state.

Personal State should answer:

> What happened to this person that changes how they react?

---

## Example Stack

### Loyal Clinic Doctor

System Relationship:

> Believer.

Location Pressure:

> Teresta-controlled clinic where treatment is tied to records and chip status.

Group Pressure:

> Doctor trained to treat procedure as protection.

Personal State:

> Their own child survived because regulated medicine worked.

Likely behavior:

> They may deny illegal treatment sincerely, not because they are cruel, but because exceptions feel dangerous to the system that saved their family.

---

### Rebel Smuggler

System Relationship:

> Rejector.

Location Pressure:

> Hidden alley network outside official records.

Group Pressure:

> Rebel supply contact who survives through secrecy.

Personal State:

> Betrayed once by someone who claimed to be escaping Regulatus.

Likely behavior:

> They may help the player, exploit them, or test them harshly before trust is possible.

---

### Ventari Official

System Relationship:

> Performer / Manipulator.

Location Pressure:

> Public ceremony where Regulatus must appear sacred.

Group Pressure:

> Ventari representative responsible for preserving public belief.

Personal State:

> Privately afraid that the old truth is becoming discoverable.

Likely behavior:

> They speak beautifully about sacrifice, order, and gratitude while carefully steering attention away from historical details that could expose House Ventari's theft.

---

## Design Rule

Do not use these labels as boxes that replace writing.

Use them as pressure tests.

If an NPC feels flat, ask:

1. What system relationship shaped them?
2. What location pressure bends their behavior?
3. What group pressure defines their role?
4. What personal state makes them specific?
5. Which part follows from experience, emotion, reason, and behavior?

If the answer cannot be found, the NPC may not yet be grounded enough.

---

## Test Warning

This model can fail if it becomes too rigid.

The goal is not to classify every person perfectly.

The goal is to make NPC behavior feel like it comes from the world instead of from quest convenience.

If future examples show that these categories are too broad, too simple, or too limiting, revise them.
