# Viriatus and the Origin of Regulatus

## Purpose

This file defines the historical origin of Regulatus and the political transformation that created the foundation of Erit's world.

It explains how Viriatus became the emotional and political proof used to reshape civilization, how his image was turned into doctrine, and how the four founding lines became the cornerstones of society.

This file should remain historical, political, social, and emotional.

Do not explain Fragments cosmology here.

---

## Locked Names

The original ruling family is **House Ventari**.

The four founding siblings are:

- Ermerus Ventari
- Rutare Ventari
- Ilunis Ventari
- Teresta Ventari

House Ventari is the family that conceals Viriatus's arrival, shapes him into a conqueror, kills him when he becomes too beloved, and formalizes Regulatus to claim his victories as inherited right.

---

## Core Principle

Regulatus is inherited legacy on the surface, but mass control underneath.

It teaches citizens to take pride in accepting their place, while quietly converting that pride into obedience, social stability, and power concentration around House Ventari and the later ruling dynasty.

From inside society, Regulatus does not feel like oppression.

It feels like:

- duty
- pride
- family honor
- gratitude
- belonging
- maturity
- protection
- continuity
- civilization

Structurally, it:

- channels obedience upward
- preserves hierarchy
- normalizes inequality
- turns conquered land into inherited right
- makes class position feel moral
- makes dissent feel shameful
- keeps power flowing toward House Ventari and the later dynasty

---

## Hidden Arrival

Viriatus falls from the heavens under circumstances House Ventari later hides.

House Ventari never publicly claims to have found him.

His true arrival is dangerous knowledge.

If people knew House Ventari simply discovered him, other families, religious authorities, military factions, or popular movements could challenge their claim over him.

They could argue that Viriatus belonged to:

- Rome
- the gods
- the people
- the army
- another noble house
- no family at all

The witnesses who saw too much are removed, punished, or erased from record.

Civel who spread rumors are killed or silenced.

House Ventari hides the arrival and sells the transformation.

---

## Viriatus As Proof

House Ventari does not publicly say:

> We found him.

They say:

> We formed him.

Viriatus is presented as proof that Ventari teaching, discipline, hierarchy, and social philosophy can shape greatness.

He becomes the proof-product of the method that later becomes Regulatus.

The public message becomes:

> Power without order is danger.  
> Order gives power purpose.  
> Viriatus proved this.  
> Regulatus preserves it.

House Ventari's claim is not:

> Follow Viriatus.

Their claim is:

> Follow the system that made Viriatus possible.

---

## Weaponizing His Wound

Viriatus comes from a world where natural family and biological inheritance have been lost.

Children are created through artificial systems rather than natural birth.

Rome gives him what his origin world could not:

- household
- language
- belonging
- legacy
- family structure
- the dream of children and continuation

This creates his deepest wound.

He wants to belong.

He wants legacy.

He wants permanence.

He wants a world where nothing precious disappears without meaning.

House Ventari learns to direct this wound outward.

They teach him that disorder is the enemy of continuity.

They teach him that conquest can be correction.

They teach him that a broken world can be made whole through order.

Viriatus does not believe he is conquering for vanity.

He believes he is helping create a civilization where loss, chaos, and disappearance can be controlled.

This makes him useful.

This also makes him dangerous.

---

## Conquest As Ownership

As Viriatus conquers, House Ventari frames every victory as proof of their method.

They claim authorship over the conqueror.

Because they claim authorship over the conqueror, they claim ownership over what he conquers.

The doctrine becomes:

> If our order made greatness, then everything greatness gained belongs to the order.

This allows conquered lands to be treated not as theft, but as rightful inheritance.

House Ventari teaches that Viriatus did not win land for himself.

He fulfilled the proof of Regulatus.

Therefore, the land belongs to the order that formed him.

The order is carried by House Ventari.

This is how conquest becomes dynastic right.

Locked principle:

> Conquest becomes ownership because Regulatus claims authorship over the conqueror.

---

## The Turning Point

As Viriatus conquers, the people begin to see him as more than a weapon.

They begin to believe he can lead civilization himself.

He becomes:

- beloved by soldiers
- feared by enemies
- admired by citizens
- mythologized by common people
- seen as the true force behind the new order

This threatens House Ventari.

House Ventari created the myth, but the myth begins to belong to Viriatus instead of them.

The weapon is becoming the ruler.

---

## The Death of Viriatus

House Ventari kills Viriatus when his personal influence becomes too great to control.

His death is not presented as betrayal.

It is rewritten as sacrifice.

The public version becomes:

> Viriatus gave himself so order could survive.

His death becomes emotional proof that civilization must be regulated.

House Ventari steals the grief surrounding him and turns it into obedience.

To question the new order becomes to dishonor Viriatus.

To obey becomes to participate in his legacy.

---

## Birth of Regulatus

Regulatus is formalized after Viriatus's death.

It is propaganda built from pride, grief, controlled memory, and inherited obligation.

Its purpose is to convert admiration for Viriatus into loyalty to the system that claims to have created him.

Regulatus teaches:

- obedience is worth
- gratitude is maturity
- hierarchy protects civilization
- accepting one's place preserves the whole
- discipline creates greatness
- personal pride comes from serving order
- family honor matters more than individual desire
- legacy must be protected through structure
- what Regulatus gains, Regulatus is owed

From inside society, Regulatus does not feel like oppression.

It feels like pride.

It feels like belonging.

It feels like family honor.

It feels like safety.

It feels like purpose.

This is why people accept it.

---

## The Four Siblings

After Viriatus dies, House Ventari needs to prevent society from centering itself around one uncontrollable figure again.

His legacy is divided into four controlled pillars.

The four Ventari siblings become the official cornerstones of society:

- Ermerus Ventari
- Rutare Ventari
- Ilunis Ventari
- Teresta Ventari

Their names later form the hidden foundation of Erit's name.

They are not sacred because they were naturally chosen.

They are made sacred because House Ventari needs human pillars through which Regulatus can survive.

Each sibling becomes a controlled interpretation of the order Viriatus supposedly made possible.

---

## Founding Line Functions

### Ermerus Ventari

Ermerus controls meaning.

His line becomes associated with doctrine, record, interpretation, education, official memory, and the moral language of Regulatus.

### Rutare Ventari

Rutare controls order.

His line becomes associated with administration, civic identity, records, permissions, bureaucracy, and the systems that decide who belongs.

### Ilunis Ventari

Ilunis controls force.

His line becomes associated with military discipline, enforcement, sacrifice, punishment, security, and violence framed as duty.

### Teresta Ventari

Teresta controls continuity.

Her line becomes associated with bloodline, household, preservation, medicine, inheritance, biological legacy, and later the systems that lead toward augmentation culture and the Devaar family.

## Regulatus As NPC Conditioning

Regulatus should later function as a civilization-wide NPC condition.

It should shape how non-rebel characters behave, speak, justify decisions, and interpret the world.

Most loyal NPCs should not sound brainwashed.

They should sound reasonable from inside their inherited worldview.

Examples:

- A clerk believes records keep families from disappearing.
- A guard believes force is ugly, but disorder is worse.
- A doctor believes treatment must follow lineage, risk, and civic priority.
- A Servan believes having a place is better than chaos.
- A noble believes privilege is inherited responsibility.
- A teacher believes gratitude must be learned early.
- A parent believes a registered child is a protected child.

NPC rule:

> Ask what Regulatus makes this person proud of.

Regulatus controls through pride more than fear.

## Condition Layers For Quest Design

World conditions should be visible through behavior, not only dialogue.

Regulatus is the global condition for most of Erit's civilization.

Local conditions can then be layered on top of it:

- fear of a ruthless ruler
- fear of dangerous wildlife outside the walls
- medical scarcity
- rebel presence
- dynastic inspection
- plague or contamination
- transport restriction
- chip failure
- identity dispute

Quest behavior should emerge from condition layers.

Formula:

> experience -> emotion -> reason -> behavior -> atmosphere -> quest logic

This prevents quests from feeling pasted onto the world.

The player should not feel:

> This NPC is acting this way because the quest needs it.

They should feel:

> Of course this person acts this way. Look at the world they live in.

## Core Historical Truth

Viriatus conquered.

House Ventari claimed authorship over him.

The people began to love him more than House Ventari.

House Ventari killed him.

Regulatus turned his memory into control.

The four Ventari siblings became the pillars that made the control permanent.

---

## Original Sin of Erit's World

Erit's world is not founded only on conquest.

It is founded on emotional theft.

A displaced child wanted family, belonging, and legacy.

House Ventari turned that wound into conquest.

When the people began loving him more than House Ventari, they killed him and turned his image into doctrine.

Regulatus is the system that transformed one person's longing for continuity into a civilization built on hierarchy, inheritance, preservation, and control.

The world still carries that wound.

Most people no longer know where it came from.
