## Why Four Eras Were Chosen

The first question was whether the world should be organized by generations or eras.

The decision was to use eras.

Generations can become messy because families overlap, lines continue, some branches disappear, and later consequences may matter more than birth order.

Eras are cleaner because they track the events that create the most historical change.

The four eras were chosen because each one marks a major transformation in how power, legitimacy, and daily life work:

1. **Founding** — the Regulatus order begins through Viriatus, House Ventari, murder, public grief, Regulatus, and AVD.
2. **Fracture** — Regulatus survives, but the Ventari founding structure fractures as the family that uses Regulatus to control the masses becomes consumed by bloodline legitimacy, disputed inheritance, and internal authority conflict.
3. **Devaar Rise** — after a time jump, the rejected Teresta line returns through medicine, preservation, chips, augmentation, and bodily dependency.
4. **Erit Era** — the consequences of those earlier changes become the lived world of Erit, Sera, Kael, House Devaar, identity control, and medical access.

The chosen structure became:

> origin -> family fracture -> return through necessity -> modern consequence

This keeps the folder structure tied to change, not just chronology.

## Why This Matters

This structure keeps the worldbuilding from spreading sideways too fast.

Each era now has a job.

The folders are not just organization.

They are a way to protect causality.

The world should continue developing through:

> Founding -> Fracture -> Devaar Rise -> Erit Era

This keeps later systems tied to earlier wounds without forcing every detail into the first generation.

It also protects House Devaar from appearing too early.

Devaar should not begin as a first founding authority.

Devaar should return later through usefulness, medicine, preservation, chips, augmentation, and bodily dependency.

That return becomes stronger because it follows rejection.
