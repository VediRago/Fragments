# Development Diary — Early Method Validation

## Context

The pressure-based design framework has now been used repeatedly across multiple areas of the project.

Originally the method was explored as a way to build NPCs.

Unexpectedly, it began producing coherent results in worldbuilding, faction design, social structures, and quest generation.

---

## Method

world pressure -> character need -> obstacle -> possible approaches -> consequence

---

## Current Observation

The framework appears to function across multiple design layers.

### Worldbuilding

Large-scale systems can be examined through pressures and consequences.

Examples:

- food shortages
- social stagnation
- gang influence
- institutional decline
- post-flare survival

These pressures naturally generate believable social conditions.

---

### Factions

Faction behavior becomes easier to explain.

Rather than defining a faction by aesthetics or ideology alone, the framework asks:

What pressure created this faction?

The answer often explains its goals and methods.

---

### NPCs

NPC creation became significantly faster.

Characters feel connected to the setting because they emerge from the same pressures affecting the wider world.

The framework shifts focus away from biography and toward motivation.

---

### Quests

Quest generation produced the strongest results.

Multiple quest seeds emerged naturally from existing world conditions.

The resulting quests felt less artificial because they originated from real problems within the setting.

---

## Design Hypothesis

The method may work because it models causality.

Pressure creates need.

Need creates conflict.

Conflict creates choices.

Choices create consequences.

Stories emerge from those consequences.

---

## Current Limitations

The framework has not yet been stress-tested extensively.

Several questions remain:

- Does it continue to work at larger scales?
- Can it generate long narrative arcs?
- Can it support major character development?
- Does it create repetitive patterns over time?
- How well does it handle exceptional individuals who reject expected outcomes?

---

## Preliminary Conclusion

Early testing suggests the framework is effective at generating coherent NPCs, quests, and social structures.

The method appears promising as a design tool.

However, additional testing is required before treating it as a core project methodology.

The next stage should focus on structured case studies and repeated validation across different parts of the setting.
